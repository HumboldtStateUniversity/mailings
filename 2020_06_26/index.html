A Message from Humboldt State University

June 26, 2020

Loss of HSU Student Mattea Roberts

Dear University Community,

We are sad to share news of the loss of Humboldt State University student Mattea Roberts. We extend our condolences to all who have been affected by her loss, particularly her family members and friends.

Mattea, a Political Science and a Critical Race & Gender Studies major, was entering her sophomore year at HSU. She died on June 25 and was 19 years old. During her freshman year, she was registered with numerous student clubs including Debate, Queer Student Union, and Women's Caucus for Art. The University has been in close contact with Mattea’s family and will continue to assist them during this tragic time.

Mattea’s death is a difficult loss for our community, especially now, when we are forced to remain socially distant from one another. Despite these circumstances, HSU is committed to supporting the needs of our university community. For those who are struggling with grief or otherwise, Counseling & Psychological Services (CAPS) is available virtually for support. If you are interested in speaking with a counselor please contact CAPS at 707-826-3236.

If you need help facilitating a conversation with your faculty or others on campus, the Dean of Students Office may be reached at 707-826-3504 or dos@humboldt.edu. Staff or faculty seeking additional support may utilize the Employee Assistance Program at 707-443-7358.
