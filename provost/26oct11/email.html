Dear Campus Community,

Next week HSU will host our 13th annual Campus Dialogue on Race. The theme for this year's Campus Dialogue is (In)Equality to Equity in Education, a topic of particular importance for our campus as we strive to achieve our goals of inclusive academic excellence.

As part of this week-long series of events, we are fortunate to have on campus Dr. Tyrone Howard of UCLA, a national expert on improving equity in educational outcomes. In addition to his keynote address, Dr. Howard will facilitate two workshops - specifically for faculty, staff and administrators - that will focus on strategies for closing the achievement gap and creating more inclusive classrooms for our students. I encourage you to participate in one of these important opportunities. More information is available below and at http://www.humboldt.edu/diversity/Download/Tyrone_Howard_flyer_10.18.11.pdf

Workshop: "Campus Dialogue on Closing the Achievement Gap."
Wednesday, November 2nd, 9-11am, Goodwin Forum
Please RSVP through the HSU Training Website

A Working Lunch: "Inclusive Pedagogy for Educators."
Wednesday, November 2nd, 12-2pm, Goodwin Forum (lunch provided)
Please RSVP through the HSU Training Website

A full schedule of events for the week-long Campus Dialogue on Race (including information on Dr. Howard's keynote address) is available at http://humboldt.edu/dialogue.

Thank you for your ongoing engagement in this critical conversation.

Robert Snyder
Provost and Vice-President for Academic Affairs
