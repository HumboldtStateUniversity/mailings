Information for Students on Possible Faculty Strike,
April 13-15 and 18-19

March 28, 2016

Dear Students,

Many of you have questions about the possible faculty strike and how it could directly impact you. Some information about the strike is provided below and additional information is in the Student FAQ.

The University also has created a website with resources and updates about the possible strike, which is available at humboldt.edu/strikeinfo.

Why could there be a strike?

The California Faculty Association (CFA) is the union to which many of our faculty belong. All faculty contract negotiations occur at the statewide level, and the CSU Chancellor’s Office and CFA have not been able to reach a salary agreement through the collective bargaining process. The CFA has announced plans for a five-day strike on all 23 CSU campuses on April 13-15 and April 18-19. The union has the right to take this action; each faculty member may choose whether or not to participate.

Will campuses be open if there is a strike?

Yes, HSU will remain open if there is a strike, and students should be able to access non-instructional programs and services without interruption, including campus-wide activities and events such as the Outstanding Student Award Ceremony on April 14. Any changes will be communicated on the strike information website at humboldt.edu/strikeinfo.

All campus entrances, buildings, and service areas should be open during normal hours of operation on the designated strike days. There will be no partial refunds of tuition and fees, housing fees, meal plan fees, or other related costs.

How will my classes be affected if there is a strike?

Some of your faculty may choose to strike and cancel classes during one or more of the five-day strike period. Others may choose not to strike and will continue to teach classes, hold office hours, and continue other normal faculty responsibilities. You should not assume that if one instructor cancels classes that this will affect other classes – even in the same department.

Faculty who do strike should inform you in advance about how that may impact your courses, including modifications to course syllabi, deferred or alternative assignments, and possible make-up work. If your instructor does not provide clear guidance about how the strike activity may impact your course, you should ask the instructor or inquire with the relevant department office.

If you are participating in off-campus course-related activities – such as student teaching, internships, or service learning – you should continue to do so.

The possible strike activity should not impact your ability to complete courses and earn full academic credit for this semester. If you have concerns about this, please contact your academic advisor.

What are my rights as a student?

The University fully respects the free speech rights of students, staff, and faculty. You are not obligated to support or oppose a possible strike. You cannot be compelled to walk out of class, join picket lines, or otherwise participate in strike activities for course-related reasons.

Any retaliation from any member of the campus community for any position you take is unlawful and inappropriate, and should be reported to the Office of Human Resources/Academic Personnel Services.

More information is available in the Student FAQ and on the strike information website at humboldt.edu/strikeinfo. For information about your specific classes, please contact your instructors first, and then, if necessary, the relevant department offices. If you cannot find the answers you need, you may contact the Office of the Vice President for Academic Affairs (Provost) or Office of the Vice President for Enrollment Management and Student Affairs.

Finally, please understand that if a strike occurs, the impact could be difficult and emotional for many members of our University community. We encourage you to remain respectful of one another, of your faculty and staff, and of the sense of community that makes Humboldt such a special place.
