A Message from Humboldt State University

May 5, 2020

Message from the Provost and Vice Provost for Academic Affairs

Dear HSU students,

We hope you and your loved ones are doing well and staying safe. As the semester comes to a close, we want to take a moment to acknowledge how amazing you all are! We know it has been a difficult adjustment - balancing academics with home life, especially during this uncertain time. We know of many examples of students overcoming great obstacles to continue your education. You inspire us.

At HSU, we have been doing our best to make this transition as smooth as possible for you. Faculty and staff shifted quickly to create a virtual campus so students could continue with classes and still receive the support they need to be successful. We changed many practices and policies to help relieve some stress for students; examples include: the credit/no credit policy which allows for most classes to be taken for CR/NC instead of a grade (deadline to do this is May 8th), the Learning Center shifted to virtual tutoring, Clubs and Activities offered and continues to offer virtual activities to keep you engaged and motivated, Financial Aid is still busily working to get students their CARES Act funds, and Advisors are working with their students to register for fall classes.

We still do not know exactly how we will be teaching and learning this Fall. We expect that decision will be coming soon. Whatever we do, rest assured we will continue to refine our teaching practices and academic policies, keeping your best interests and the quality of your educational experience in the forefront of our decisions.

Below are some quick links to resources available to you. Campus offices will be open throughout the summer as well. We are here for you - now, and in the future.

  • HSU's Covid-19 Frequently Asked Questions
  • Campus Resources are available
  • OhSNAP! is available and has free pre-packaged food pick-ups on campus
  • HSU's Keep Learning site is the go-to resource to keep checking as we make the transition to virtual classes and services.
  • The Learning Center https://learning.humboldt.edu for virtual tutoring and learning support.

Good luck during finals. Congratulations to all of our graduates. We hope you stay safe and healthy and your summer provides some much-needed rest and rejuvenation.

Sincerely,

Dr. Mary Oling-Sisay
