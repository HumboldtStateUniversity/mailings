Working for California
Three HSU Alumni are featured in the new Working for California booklet published by the California State University. The publication, along with an accompanying website and display at the Capitol, celebrate the numerous contributions California State University alumni have made to California. These folks have made big impacts in every field, and our own Richard Cuneo ('62, Business Administration), Stephen Hillenburg ('84, Marine Biology) and Michael Crooke ('86, '89, Forestry) are recognized for their contributions to California's agricultural economy and popular culture. There is no shortage of inspiring HSU alumni who are making the world a better place, and this new publication honors their work. For more inspiring alumni take a look at HSU's Distinguished Alumni award recipients.

You can read more about Cuneo, Crooke and Hillenburg, along with other great CSU alumni at http://www.calstate.edu/workingforca/.

Alumnus To Deliver Commencement Address
This year's Commencement keynote address will be delivered by Humboldt State alumnus Robert Thomas ('85, Geology), Professor of Geology at University of Montana-Western, Dillon. Thomas was named 2009 U.S. Professor of the Year by the Council for Advancement and Support of Education and was one of HSU's 2008 HSU Distinguished Alumni. Read more about Thomas and HSU's May 14 commencement »

Thomas will discuss the importance of education amid tight budgets and resurgent anti-intellectualism. We'll be sure to include a full transcript of his speech in next month's Alumni Newsletter.
