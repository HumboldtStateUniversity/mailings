Lend a hand with alumni scholarships
We're looking for a few alumni to help us pick the recipients of the 2011 Alumni Association Scholarships. Scholarship recipients are selected on their academic achievement, activities and need. No meetings are required, just access to email and a willingness to review applications. If this sounds like something you'd like to help with, drop us a line at alumni@humboldt.edu.

Speed Networking in Humboldt
Alumni volunteers in the Humboldt area are needed to participate in a Speed Networking mixer in Arcata on Tuesday, April 26, which will provide students and alumni with a dynamic opportunity to engage with each other in a structured format. Students get live experiences that help them learn how to pitch themselves and make a good first impression. Alumni have the opportunity to talk about their post-college job experience and provide possible employer or industry contacts. Everyone will have some fun! If you're interested, please email Kim (alumni@humboldt.edu) at Humboldt Alumni.

Campus Hosts Rick Rosenthal, Acclaimed Marine Life Cinematographer—and we've
got tickets
Rick Rosenthal, HSU alumnus ('67), marine biologist and two-time Emmy Award winning cinematographer, is coming to HSU for a day of workshops and presentations from his most recent work. Rosenthal's presentation takes place at 7 p.m., April 19, in the Van Duzer Theatre.

Rosenthal's work includes the BBC landmark series Blue Planet, and the feature film Deep Blue, on which he was principal cameraman. Rosenthal has also worked on the PBS documentary series Superfish, the blockbuster series Planet Earth and Disney Nature's Earth.

We're excited to announce we have 50 tickets available to HSU alumni on a first-come, first-serve basis. Give us a call at (707) 826-3312 to reserve your ticket. All tickets must be picked up in person from the Humboldt Alumni office in Nelson Hall East 112.
