Donors Help Preserve Football

Alumni and community donors rallied for Humboldt State, raising funds to help HSU continue its intercollegiate football team. “We’re proud of our athletics program and our football team, and I’m so thankful for those who have stepped up to provide the financial resources to continue all our sports,” says HSU President Lisa Rossbacher.

Making Spirits Bright

Nothing says the holidays like shiny bows and pretty paper. Last month, Winter Wishes volunteers came together to wrap gifts worth more than $9,000 and deliver them to 93 local children in need. Thanks to everyone in the Humboldt State community for their generosity and support. You put smiles on many faces.

Class Notes

Ruben Isaí Madriz Villanueva, 2007 Biological Sciences and Zoology, embarked on a two-year-long bicycle trip through the Americas during which he rode from Chicago to the southern tip of South America, volunteering along the way at wildlife rehabilitation centers and parks. He then attended UNAM in Mexico City and received a master’s degree in electron microscopy. In 2011 he married HSU alumna, Kristina Lindsay (2006, Women’s Studies). They moved from California to Iowa where he earned a Ph.D. in Entomology from Iowa State. He and Kristina are currently living in Patagonia, Chile where he is a Fulbright-National Geographic Digital Storytelling Fellow focused on rare insects.
