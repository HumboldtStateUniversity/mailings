College Fairs

Alumni Hit The Road

Every year Humboldt State hits the road to recruit new students at college fairs. These potential students enjoy hearing from alumni who tell them about all the great things HSU has to offer—like access to amazing outdoor settings, hands-on learning, and incredible professors.

Here’s a big thank you to all the alumni volunteers who helped out this year: Clara Trigg Parker, Frances Harris, Mike Voss, Michael Sagehorn, David Kalb, Ona Lee Kimber, and Steve Aberlee.

Representing HSU in your hometown is easy. All you need to do is answer questions or share info on social media. Learn more online.

Volunteer Opportunity

Welcome Potential Students at HSU's Spring Preview: March 25 & April 1

Forever Humboldt is seeking local alumni interested in participating in a panel to address potential students and their parents attending Spring Preview. The event takes place on Friday, March 25, and Friday, April 1, from 1:45 to 2:30 p.m. at HSU. Alumni are asked to share memories of HSU, plus tips to succeed in school, and what to expect after graduation.

Latest Class Notes

William Roberts, 1967 Theatre Arts, followed his undergraduate education with degrees from UC Riverside and the University of Manchester, where he took his diploma in Drama in 1969. Roberts spent five years as an actor and director with the Oregon Shakespeare Festival from 1963 to 1972. He has been a resident of Britain and a member of the British Actor’s Equity since the late 1960s.

A broadly experienced actor and voice artiste, he is a frequent reader of American works on BBC Radio and for various audio publishers. He is often heard voicing “heavy” characters in major video games. His film and television appearances include “Behind Closed Doors,” “Infinite Justice,” “Cold Mountain,” and “Mission: Impossible—Rogue Nation.” Roberts recently published two novellas set on the North Coast, where he was raised. “The Humanist,” a mystery novel, is set in Shelter Cove in Mendocino County. His second book, “An Ill Wind,” takes place in Del Norte County, with scenes set in San Francisco, L.A., and Puerto Vallarta, Mexico. He is also a private pilot based in London.

Robert Deane, 2007 Journalism, is currently the Library Technician/Paraprofessional Aide for the Butte Valley Unified School District. He also completed his second year as the school's JV Boys Basketball coach, winning the Evergreen League title with a 6-0 League Record. This summer he'll begin his teaching credential program at Simpson University.

Crystal Schalmo, 2004 Biological Sciences, participated in Miami University’s Earth Expeditions global field course in Costa Rica during Summer 2015. There, Schalmo studied biotic, physical, and cultural forces that affect tropical biodiversity at the Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve and La Selva Biological Station. A senior keeper at the San Diego Zoo Global in San Diego, Calif., she took the graduate course in pursuit of her master’s degree from Miami University’s Global Field Program.
