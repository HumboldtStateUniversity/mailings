Heading Away to Humboldt

More than 400 new HSU students, their families, and alumni came out to four Student Send-off Picnics in Oakland, Sacramento, Anaheim, and San Diego. The picnics helped welcome new students before they make their way to campus and gave them the chance to hear from alumni about their experience at HSU. Family members also met alumni and connected with other HSU families.

Latest Class Notes

Colin Gallagher, Natural Resources, 1997, went into the Peace Corps after Humboldt graduation of 1997. He came back to the U.S in 2000. He worked various local government gigs, picked up a master’s degree along the way. He got into crypto and helped design cryptocurrencies and electronic wallets in a manner so as to foster efficient microdonations in the early years of decentralized cryptos (2009 - 2015). He did a software QA gig. He is currently working for a crypto-fiat exchange and enjoying life.

Dave Feliz, Wildlife, 1983, is the Reserve Manager for the Elkhorn Slough National Estuarine Research Reserve in Monterey County, California. This is one of 30 reserves around the nation, set up to conduct research, habitat restoration and education on lands adjacent to and including estuarine habitats. These are all state/federal partnerships with the National Oceanic Atmospheric Administration partnering with state entities. The state partner at the Elkhorn Slough is the California Department of Fish and Wildlife. Dave has been working for the department since 1984, managing lands in various parts of California.

Beverly Ferne Wasson, Physical Education, 1969, Teaching Credential, 1970, taught high school PE in the Mt. Diablo School District from 1970 to 1980. She started the first co-ed PE classes. From 1980 to 2011 she grew wine grapes on her family’s vineyards. She was the first woman to head the county farm bureau and was on the North Coast Regional Water Quality Board from 1999 to 2006. She retired and lives in the family home in Alexander Valley Sonoma County, California.
