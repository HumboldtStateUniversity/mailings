HSU Celebrates ‘New Century’ Class

See all of this year’s commencement photos on Flickr »

For photos by graduates and guests, search #hsugrad on Instagram or go here http://iconosquare.com/tag/hsugrad/

Humboldt State’s ‘New Century’ Class received diplomas in three ceremonies in Redwood Bowl. Thousands of graduates and their friends and family members packed the arena, as President Rollin Richmond, who will be retiring at the conclusion of the 2013/14 Academic Year, delivered commencement addresses to all three Colleges. Rep. Jared Huffman addressed the College of Natural Resources & Sciences ceremony.

Among those honored was Herrmann Spetzler ('87) who received an honorary Doctorate of Humane Letters for his longtime contributions to rural health care in California.

Remembering Those Lost in the I-5 Charter Bus Accident

On April 10, 2014, the HSU community was devastated by the news of a bus crash that took the lives of five high school students, an HSU admissions counselor, two chaperones and the drivers of both vehicles.

Immediately after the tragedy, alumni, faculty members, staff and students sought ways to show their support. One result is a pair of scholarships recognizing the victims’ dedication to expanding access to education. They are the Michael Myvett and Mattison Haywood Memorial Scholarship, which honors the two alumni chaperones, and the Arthur Arzola Memorial Scholarship, which recognizes the HSU admissions counselor.

To date, the funds have received more than $100,000 total in contributions and matching funds from the HSU Advancement Foundation, and they continue to grow.

More information about the I-5 Charter Bus accident and the scholarships is at humboldt.edu/busaccident.

Michael Sandecki, 1979 Geology, is currently a water quality analyst employed with the California Coastal Commission.

William Forbes, 1990 Geography, is an Associate Professor of Geography at another lumberjack school, Stephen F. Austin State University, located in the Pineywoods of East Texas. At the University of North Texas, he did his dissertation on revisiting Mexico's Rio Gavilan, where Aldo Leopold noted perfect land health in the 1930s. Forbes currently directs SFA's Center for a Livable World, which studies sustainability and livability of small cities.
