Giving Back

Economics Alum Changing Lives

When Don Lewis ('66, Economics) headed to college, he never imagined a life-long career in Economics—until a special professor changed the course of his life. Arriving at Humboldt State with the intention of majoring in Physical Education, Don took an Economics class from Dr. Ted Ruprecht. Ted saw a budding Economist and encouraged Don to rethink his major. “Dr. Ruprecht changed my whole life. I fell in love with economics as a discipline and went on to have a very enjoyable academic career as an Economist,” Don says.

Through a new charitable gift arrangement with the Humboldt State University Advancement Foundation, Don is now changing lives himself.

Don recently established the Dr. Ted Ruprecht Research Assistantship in Economics initiative with a $25,000 gift commitment.

Bragging About HSU

HSU a Leader in Producing Peace Corps Volunteers

Alumni from HSU are making big impacts all over the globe, thanks in part to outsized participation in the Peace Corps.

This year, HSU was the No. 7 medium-sized school sending alumni into the international volunteer agency. HSU ranked no. 25 among all schools nationwide.

Currently 25 alumni are serving, and since the program’s inception, a total of 814 have served.

The story even caught the attention of local NBC affiliate, KIEM-TV. Check out their coverage online.

You can read HSU’s coverage of the recent Peace Corps ranking at Humboldt State NOW.

Latest Class Notes

Meg Godlewski, 1987 Journalism, has combined journalism talent with a passion for aviation. She is celebrating 16 years as the staff reporter for General Aviation News, a national magazine. Godlewski also supplies the Ask the CFI column for Aviation for Women magazine. Godlewski is a frequent contributor to aviation textbooks, combining humor with science, and technique. Godlewski is anticipating the publication of her first fiction novel sometime this summer, if she can work the edits and rewrite around a busy flying schedule.

Evan Lautzenheiser, 2003 Wildlife, worked for Green Diamond as a wildlife technician conducting spotted owl surveys after graduating. Lautzenheiser also worked with desert bighorn sheep in Palm Springs, California. In 2007 he became a district wildlife manager/game ranger for Arizona Game and Fish.
