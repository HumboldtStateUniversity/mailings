Latest Class Notes

Deanna Dick (‘64, Zoology)
Dick had a 30 year career in Clinical Laboratory Science working in hospital and outpatient laboratories in California and Washington. She retired from Humboldt Central Laboratory in 2000, but returned in 2016. She then worked for United Indian Health Services traveling from Arcata to Weitchpec, Klamath, and Fortuna, before retiring again in 2018.

Don Treasure (‘74, Wildlife)
Treasure spent 37 years working for and with the federal government as a Biological Technician, Environmental Protection Specialist, and Environmental Specialist. He is now fully retired.

Gary Coyne (‘73, Oceanography)
Coyne worked for 40 years as a scientific glassblower, with over 30 years at Cal State University, Los Angeles. While working at CSULA he wrote the book “The Laboratory Companion” and had a wonderful career helping make both the glass apparatus used in general labs and many one-of-a-kind apparatus used by chemists, geologists, biologists, and physicists. He retired in 2015 and since then has gone deeper with his hobby of woodworking and has spent more time on his bike. He also helps Adobe in their forums answering questions on things like Lightroom, Photoshop, Acrobat, and other related programs. In addition, he still provides support to the American Scientific Glassblowers Society.
