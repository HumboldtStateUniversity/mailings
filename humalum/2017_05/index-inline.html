HSU Honors 2017 Distinguished Alumni Award Winners!

Left to right: Kellie Johnson, Ken Pimlott, and
Michael Rains


Youtube: 2017 HSU Honors Dinner Highlights

Kellie Johnson (’93, Political Science) Kellie Johnson has had a long career fighting for social justice and equality, having having served most recently as assistant disciplinary counsel at the Oregon State Bar.

Ken Pimlott (’88, Forest Resource Management) Ken Pimlott is currently the director of the California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection, the highlight of a career that began 33 years ago as a reserve firefighter in Contra Costa County, California.

Michael T. Rains (’68, Forestry Management, ’71 Watershed Management) A widely respected leader in linking environmental health with community stability, Michael Rains led a career that spanned nearly half a century with the U.S. Department of Agriculture’s Forest Service.

Remembering Student David Josiah Lawson

The HSU community recently came together to celebrate the life of 19-year-old student David Josiah Lawson, who was killed Saturday, April 15. Family members and those who knew Josiah remember him as a caring and charismatic person. “I’m sad and I’m heartbroken but I know that the last two years that he was here, he was loved. And I see it from everyone that’s here,” said his mother Michelle-Charmaine Lawson. Josiah and his family remain in our thoughts.

HSU Downtown Brings Students to Arcata’s Hotspots

Over the last year, Forever Humboldt made a big effort to connect students with the broader community through ‘HSU Downtown.’ It included hundreds of students dancing the night away at three DJ events at the Arcata Theatre Lounge and Humboldt Brews, and six evenings of new-release movies at the Minor Theater. All events were free due in part to alumni support.
