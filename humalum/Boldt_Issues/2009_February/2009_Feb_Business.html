Meet one of the “100 most influential people in business ethics!”

The School of Business presents Dr. James O’Toole at the 16th Annual Chung-Watson Lecture Series in Business Ethics on March 5, 2009, from 4-6 pm at the Kate Buchanan Room at Humboldt State University.

Dr. O’Toole will speak on “Transparency:  How Leaders Create a Culture of Candor,” which is also the topic of his recent book. This event is free to the public and a book signing will follow the event.

Dr. Mortazavi, Chair of the School of Business and Academic Senate states:  “This lecture might be of interest to the entire campus community as it addresses one of the areas identified in the Keeling & Associates report.”

Dr. O’Toole is the Daniels Distinguished Professor of Business Ethics at University of Denver’s Daniels College of Business.  O’Toole’s research and writings have been in the areas of leadership, philosophy, ethics, and corporate culture.  He has addressed dozens of major corporations and professional groups, and has over one hundred published articles.

In 2007, he was named one of the “100 most influential people in business ethics” by editors of Ethisphere, and one of “the top 100 thought leaders on leadership” by Leadership Excellence magazine.

The Chung-Watson Lecture Series in Business Ethics is supported by 1970  Humboldt alumnus Mr. Po Chung to honor Frank Watson, former HSU professor.  Mr. Po Chung, founder and past Chairman & CEO of DHL International, provided funding for this lecture series so that our students, faculty, and community would have a forum in which to engage in a continuing dialogue  on business ethics and corporate responsibility.  We are pleased to provide that forum.

Contact the Department of Business for more information.
