Alumnus and Professor Bike and Surf California Coast

January 28, 2009 – Michael Bruner

Considering that the 2009 Relay for Life events are scheduled for May 8-9 at Eureka High School and July 11-12 at College of the Redwoods, it is worthwhile recalling and, perhaps, duplicating a special event from last year.  Over a period of nine days in May 2008, Mike Conway, an HSU alumnus and former Director of Development for Surfrider Foundation Santa Barbara Chapter, and Dr. Scott Paynton, HSU professor of Organizational Communication, biked and surfed from Ft. Bragg to Santa Barbara, a distance of 580 miles.

The purpose of this journey was to highlight two issues: Cancer Research and Environmental Awareness.  Mike’s father died from cancer in 2007.  Mike and Scott decided to raise money for the American Cancer Society, while educating others about biking and surfing in healthy and environmentally friendly ways.

The pair towed all of their surf gear on a surfboard trailer made by Quiver Kaddy and hauled their supplies in an equipment trailer made by Equinox Trailers.  Other sponsors included: Teva, Simple Shoes, Home Blown eco-friendly surfboards, Chrome, Finisterre, Pacsafe, DocksLocks, ArmorBags, Wave Tribe, Endura, Matunas Eco wax, Camelbak, Hobie Sunglasses, Princeton Tec, Walkabout Travel Gear, Matuse Wetsuits, Logon Business Systems, and Big Sur River Inn.

The journey received media coverage in SurfShot Magazine and the Santa Barbara Independent.  Members of the broader HSU community can find additional details at the Web site that Mike and Scott established to provide information, photos, daily updates, and their reflections (see www.bikeandsurfcalifornia.com).
