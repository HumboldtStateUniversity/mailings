Class Notes from the Graduates of "Humboldt State College"


As we prepare to welcome our Golden Graduates as they return to campus for Homecoming, we wanted to share their memories of their time at Humboldt.

Darrel Cooley, Wildlife Management, Class of '56
Humboldt Memories: Meeting new friends and future wife. The 8 a.m. wildlife classes with Dr. Yocom in the metal Quonset huts - first one in turns on the heat! Beach parties at College Cove. Football games and drive-in movies in the rain and fog. Smoke and falling ash from the sawmill sawdust burners. No appointments needed to talk to the Registrar or advisors. And much, much more.
Larry Flammer, Zoology, Class of '56
Humboldt Memories: Met my wife to be, Sally Conklin, in my senior year, January of ’56, engaged in March, married June 16, 1957 (after a year of graduate work at the U of Washington). We just celebrated our 50th wedding anniversary in June. I look back with pride at the Popera Dance celebration that my great, hardworking committee pulled off so well. Fantastic committee, with Dean Don Karshner, and a most successful Freshman Orientation. My eldest grandson, Kurt Flammer, will be starting his Freshman year at HSU this fall.
Wesley E. Green, Sr., Education, Class of '56
Humboldt Memory: I am thankful for my education. It really helped me a lot and made my life a happy one. I really enjoyed attending Humboldt.
Carolyn (Call) Haft, Education, Class of '57
Humboldt Memories: As I recall the two enjoyable years I spent as a Humboldt State student, I appreciate more deeply HSU's important impact upon my life.

I remember the warm, friendly reception I received from Humboldt's administrators, faculty, and students after transfering to Humboldt my junior year, and I recall the pleasure of being a song queen, a member of homecoming courts and a student teacher at Sunny Brae Elementary School under the excellent guidance of Eleanor Kellenberger. Without a doubt, however, my most significant memory is meeting fellow HSU student, Happy Haft, my future husband.

As a Golden Grad also celebrating a Golden Wedding Anniversary, it is obvious that attending Humboldt was a positive, life-changing experience for me. An experience filled with unforgettable memories.
Happy Haft, Radio/Television/Speech, Class of '56
Humboldt Memories: As a student athlete from the state of Washington, I was immediately impressed with the athletic coaches, athletes, and beautiful Spanish-styled buildings on campus. Many of my favorite memories are associated with athletics. I remember my three coaches, Phil Sarboe, Franny Givins, and Ced Kinzer. I also remember the outstanding athletes I met while playing football, basketball, and baseball at HSU; memories of the many experiences we shared as teammates and friends remain an important reminder of Humboldt and its athletic programs.

My most treasured memory is meeting and marrying my college sweetheart, Carolyn. While enjoying the Golden Grad Plus reunion featuring the classes of 1956 and 1957, Carolyn and I are also celebrating fifty years of marriage.
Joyce R. Jacobs, Elementary Education, Class of '56
Humboldt Memory: Having friends check us into Nelson Hall after the SnoBall and throw jeans, etc. down to us so we could go steelhead fishing with our escorts up on the Smith River.

Lynn Pryer, Speech/Drama, Class of '56
Humboldt Memories: Pop Jenkins putting his hands on mine to help me throw a pot. Reese Bullen running into a pot he had thrown, chipping it and then telling me to throw it away, even though I didn't think the chip was obvious. Dr. Karshner having me reblock a scene over and over. John Van Duzer lecturing me about lighting gels. Kate Buchanan having a group of us over to her lovely house for tea.
Lewis E. Sbrana, Music Education, Class of '56
Humboldt Memories: Sitting in the HSU stands with the Lumberjack Band and watching the punted ball disappear in the fog. Marching down the field at halftime to the strains of "Onward Ever, HSC." The good memories of Charlie Fulkerson and Lee Barlow and the other music department profs.
Jaqueline Sintic, Social Science, Class of '57
Humboldt Memories: Memories of Humboldt include singing in the A Capella Choir and Madrigal Singers, wearing the funny wings of a fairy in "Iolanthe" by G&S; being a "Spoke"; marching proudly with the Lumberjack Band as a majorette, and many more activities---so much fun! I especially remember the rigorous classes of Drs. Palais, Spaid, Corbett, Hall, MacGinitie, Barlow, and Fulkerson and many more. I truly received a great foundation that has prepared me for a successful life. I look forward to visiting with former classmates.
