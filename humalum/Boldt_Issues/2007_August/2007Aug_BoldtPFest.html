Paddlefest Celebrates Five Years of Success

The Humboldt Bay Paddlefest, an event conceived by the Humboldt State University Alumni Association, is celebrating its 5th year of success this fall on September 29-30.  A premier West Coast paddling event, Paddlefest has served a multitude of purposes, from celebrating the recreational use of scenic Humboldt Bay to demonstrating HSU’s overall commitment to a community-based event.  Many alumni volunteer and participate in Paddlefest every year, and the event draws paddling enthusiasts from as far away as Seattle/WA, Portland/OR, Redding/CA, and the Bay Area. 

This year’s event features two days of activities showcasing Humboldt Bay and serves as a Grand Opening for HSU’s new Humboldt Bay Aquatic Center.  The Aquatic Center will serve as the official headquarters for Paddlefest, including hosting land-based activities/classes.  Water-based activities are scheduled to launch from three docks located directly north of the facility (adjacent to Halvorson Park).  Saturday’s program includes a canoe and kayak fun race around Woodley Island, children’s activities, live music, clinics and classes, vendor demos and booths, and delicious food and beverages.  Sunday, the event continues with the addition of a wacky Kardboard Kayak Race, the KEET Ducky Race (fundraiser for kid’s programs), and a host of other water-based activities.  Awards will be presented to race and contest participants both days.

Admission to Paddlefest is FREE.  Last year, more than 1500 people attended, including Humboldt State University alumni, students, local and regional paddling enthusiasts and novices, and community spectators. This year’s event (presented by the Alumni Association and Center Activities) will be partnered with the Harbor District’s Maritime Expo (an event tied closely to the city of Eureka and the U.S. Coast Guard), resulting in a more diverse range of activities for all participants to enjoy.  For additional information on Paddlefest, go to www.humboldtbaypaddlefest.com or call 707-826-3357.
