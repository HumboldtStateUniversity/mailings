Lumberjack Football Gears Up for
Tough Season

Photos courtesy of HSU Athletics

The ninety players on Humboldt State’s newly-minted football team have their work cut out for them…and then some. For starters, the team must live up to some very high expectations. Last year’s team fought long and hard for their stellar 9-1 season. In fact, 2006 proved the best football season in over thirty years. 

If that weren’t enough, consider that the Lumberjacks lost 23 seniors to caps, gowns and diplomas—many of them major contributors to last season’s success. With last year’s seniors out of the picture, this year’s team will be an entirely new machine—on both offense and defense.

Oh, and then there’s this year’s schedule to consider…. The Lumberjacks are opening the 2007 season with four straight road games. Their inaugural game? Even before the Jacks set foot in the University of North Dakota stadium on August 23rd to play the Fighting Sioux, it was clear to everyone that the Jacks’ first outing might just prove their toughest. 

The University of North Dakota is, simply put, one of the top Division-II programs in the country with three All-Americans on offense alone, which largely explains the lopsided outcome of the game: a 59-0 victory against our Jacks. As head coach Doug Adkins noted prior to the match-up, “I think the only weakness in (North Dakota’s) program might be that the lady selling popcorn isn’t very quick.”

The schedule hardly gets much easier for our team during the next few weeks. The Jacks will soon play Mesa State, Central Washington, and Menlo College. Finally, on September 29th, the Jacks will get a chance to ditch the buses and play in front of a hometown crowd when they match up against Dixie College, here in the Redwood Bowl.

HSU will be home the following two weeks, hosting Western Oregon on October 6th and Azusa Pacific on October 13th, before hitting the road again, this time to play Dixie College once again.

Want to stay in touch with how the Lumberjacks are faring this season? Each game will be aired on Bicoastal Media’s ESPN Radio affiliate, KATA in Eureka —just turn the dial on your radio to 1340 AM. Former Humboldt State football standout and broadcast journalism graduate, J. B. Mathers will give the play by plays and will be joined in each telecast by HSU Hall of Famer Burt Nordstrom. 

If the whole radio broadcast thing feels terribly outdated to you (there is, after all, an entire generation who thinks “AM” simply means “early in the morning”), you can also tune into the games via the Internet. All games will also be simulcast on the Net and can be accessed through a link on the football page of the hsujacks.com web site.  Broadcasts begin 15 minutes prior to scheduled game time with the pre-game show and will feature both interviews and analysis.
