Distinguished Alums Partner in New York Times Best Seller Quest

By Kevin Daum ('86)

There I was, 43, enjoying life in Manhattan, kayak fishing in New England on the weekends, nearly debt free.  I had done all the right things for 20 years, built an Inc. 500 company established myself as an expert in my field.  Heck, I even authored “Building Your Own Home For Dummies” and was named Distinguished Alum for HSU. When Boom! Everything went bust!  In less than 6 months my entire world disappeared. IndyMac and Lehman were my largest investors.  I was done!

That was my life in Spring 2008. I had to scramble.  I had a son getting ready for college. On the one hand I was angry!  How could this happen?!?  Not my fault!!! Next was the brutal realization that I didn’t even know how to find a job and being a typical entrepreneur I was completely unemployable.  I had to take stock of the resources I had and find a way to support my family, and retain my self-respect.

Over the years I had been the chief marketer for my company and had consulted in arts/business and marketing mostly for fun.  Relying on the support of my friends and a do anything approach learned from an HSU degree in Theatre Arts, I was able to leverage my writing ability, knowledge, experience and passion to build a Customer Experience/Marketing consultancy.   I quickly sold a new book Green$ense: Rating the Real Payoff for 50 Green Home Projects to The Taunton Press for release in 2010 and started networking to increase my clientele.

I had never networked with HSU alumni much because I wasn’t sure where the value would be living in the East and not being in Natural Sciences.   But looking through an issue of the Bolt I stumbled across Ken Fisher as another Distinguished Alum.  Ken is also an entrepreneur and we had written for the same publisher.  I didn’t know what a relationship with a best selling author and billionaire would bring but I figured he was someone it couldn’t hurt to know.  The question for myself was whether or not I could bring value to him.

I reached out to Ken.  It took some doing to get through the gatekeepers but due to my Distinguished Alum status and our common interests he granted me a rare meeting—I really think were it not for that and the prior relationship with Wiley I wouldn’t have gotten through but it was worth a shot.  I went into that meeting with no agenda other than to make a connection with someone who shared a love for HSU.  We spoke for a while and discussed our paths.  We found common ground in our desire to see HSU alumni gain value from involvement and connection to the university.  We started working on an HSU project that fell victim to budget cuts but gained mutual respect for each other.

In December 2008 I wrote a charming parable ROAR! Get Heard in The Sales and Marketing Jungle, a unique story that identifies a 3500-year-old sales process direct from the Jewish Passover seder.  The book had some traction in the business world and I sent it to Ken to see what he thought.  He told me liked it and was kind enough to pen an endorsement for the cover.  When it became clear that the book had real potential Ken offered to take it to Wiley.  I had a Wiley relationship from my Dummies book but I knew Ken’s influence would matter and it did.  Even Ken won’t get Wiley to buy a bad book but in this difficult publishing environment I know it was the support of an influential alumni relationship that made Wiley take a good look.

Wiley has now picked up the book ROAR! to release in April and we are on the path to being a New York Times bestseller or as I like to call it... The Quest for the Jewish Super Bowl Ring!   ROAR is already attracting clients and a big following in it’s pre-release. Of course it didn’t hurt that I gained some press attention by tattooing New York Times Best Seller on my chest backwards so I could see my goal in the mirror every morning.  I am learning much from Ken’s publishing success and I hope Ken is  learning some new tricks from my theatrical approach as well.

I’m a strong believer in the ties that we have from shared experience and the experience of attending Humboldt State is a powerful one that lasts a lifetime.  When my son was picking colleges I told him that the alumni program was probably the most important factor in choosing the school.  For now I know first hand that alumni connections can have impact throughout your life and career.  Few have a story compelling enough to engage a billionaire but there are thousands of HSU alumni who will network freely and can help each other. All you have to do is reach out.

Kevin Daum (Theatre Arts, 86) is the principal of TAE International and author of several books including his latest, “ROAR Get heard in the Sales and Marketing Jungle”. (Wiley) You can support Kevin’s Quest for the Jewish Super Bowl Ring at www.AwesomeRoar.com.  You can reach out to him at Kevin@TheAwesomeExperience.com.
