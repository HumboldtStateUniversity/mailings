Alumnus Joins Team Studying Haiti Quake

HSU Geology alum Richard Koehler (MS '99), has been approved by the Alaska Governor’s office to join a team of scientists for two weeks in Haiti and the Dominican Republic to assist in mapping and evaluating the fault rupture associated with the January 12 M7 earthquake. Rich was asked by team leader Paul Mann of the University of Texas at Austin to participate in this NSF-funded rapid-response effort requested by the Haitian government. He left Saturday, January 23, 2010.

Koehler’s invitation was partly based on his field experience working along the same fault zone in Jamaica prior to his employment with the Alaska Division of Geological & Geophysical Surveys. One goal of the rapid-response team is to assess any continuing motions along the fault that could lead to future large earthquakes in the region, possibly in Jamaica. Koehler’s experience there will be directly applicable to his work evaluating active faults in Alaska, as several faults such as the Denali, Castle Mountain, Lake Clark, and Fairweather have rupture styles similar to the fault system in Haiti.
