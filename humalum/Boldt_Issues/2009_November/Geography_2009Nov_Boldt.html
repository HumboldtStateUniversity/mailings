Humboldt State Students, Alums, Dominate National Map Competition

An HSU Geography Alumnus and an HSU Undergraduate Student Took First and Second Place in the North American Cartographic Information Society (NACIS) 2009 Student Map Competition, held in Sacramento, California this month.

Current Humboldt State undergraduate geography major, Diana Muncy, won second place in the competition, behind First Place winner, HSU Alum Mike Boruta '08 (now a Master's student at University of Ohio).  

The field was challenging.  It included fifteen graduate and undergraduate entrants from Humboldt State University, the University of Wisconsin, Ohio University, and Pennsylvania State University, among others.

Over 200 conference participants gathered from North America, Europe, and New Zealand to meet October 6th-9th in Sacramento for the 29th Annual NACIS Conference.

The Humboldt State Geography Department–past and present–was well represented at the meetings.  Alumni Kevin McManigal (Adventure Cycling Cartographer) and Nathaniel Vaughn Kelso (Washington Post Cartographer) gave presentations during "Practical Cartography Day," while Cassie Hansen (PhD student at University of Nevada), Miles Eggleston (North Coast Journal Cartographer), and Nick Ramirez (MA student at HSU) joined current HSU geography undergraduates Jaimie Baxter, Julian LoGiudici, Jemma Williams, and Professor Dennis Fitzsimons as participants.

HSU Lab Manager/Lecturer Mary Beth Cunha served as Local Arrangements Coordinator and Board Member for the conference, while HSU Geography Professor Stephen Cunha led a Saturday field trip to Lake Tahoe for conference attendees. 

Humboldt State University cartographers have become a recognized presence on the national scene, often winning in competitions with cartographic powerhouses such as the University of Kansas, Pennsylvania State University, and the University of Wisconsin.  HSU's state-of-the-art Kosmos cartography and visualization laboratory–established with National Science Foundation funding–along with excellent instruction, has established Humboldt State University as one of the nation's premier centers for mapping sciences
