Looking Back at Homecoming 2007


Alumni, families, and students were treated to some beautiful Humboldt weather this fall for Homecoming Weekend. The HSU Alumni Association teamed up with Student Affairs to pack the weekend with events and expand the Homecoming theme to include activities that would interest returning alumni.

Festivities kicked off Friday at noon with the Association's bi-annual Golden Grad Luncheon celebrating the classes of 1956 and 1957. More than 25 golden graduates returned to Humboldt, some from as far away as North Carolina and Alabama. Happy '56 and Carolyn '57 Haft were the honorary chairs and provided a rousing presentation and nostalgic look back to many special memories of Humboldt State. More»

Later that afternoon, alumni joined a crowd of more than 200 at the dedication of the new Behavioral and Social Sciences (BSS) building. President Richmond and former President Alistair MaCrone led the ceremony, which included Native American blessings and performances by HSU’s renowned Calypso Band. More»

As evening rolled around, alums chose from a variety of activities, including an alumni reception, theatre, symphony, Athletics' Hall of Fame dinner, and socializing. The Hall of Fame inductees included; Shelli Maher Sarchett, Taiisha Pleasant and Megan Keesling, who helped lead the Jacks to softball glory, men's soccer standout Ian Danielson, football star Mike Mari, women's basketball player Carrie LaBudde, and the 1977 wrestling team.

Alums spent the morning visiting the Farmer's Market, First Street Gallery, the forest, marsh or beach, or enjoying an old time “lumberjack breakfast” at the Samoa Cookhouse. Then hundreds of alums, families, and students returned to campus for the Lumberjack BBQ before heading to the Redwood Bowl for the Homecoming game. Although the Jacks did not prevail over Azusa Pacific, a good time was had by all!

The Alumni Association gave away a Jacks megaphone to everyone who stopped by their booth. They also hosted a halftime drawing for an iPod Nano and two "alumni warmer kits." (All giveaways were sponsored by Liberty Mutual.) Congratulations to the winners -- John Berger '61 and Lynn Huynn '11 won alumni warmer kits and Velma Childs Titus '45 won an iPod Nano! Velma claimed her prize at our table…and her excited granddaughter was thrilled to become the recipient of this surprise cool gift!

The weekend closed with "Brunch at the J."

It was rewarding for us to see so many alumni return for Homecoming, and we hope to convince more of you to return next year! Make plans to return to Humboldt for Homecoming 2008…tentatively scheduled for October 4th. Stay tuned for more.
