Five HSU Students to Participate in Acting Competition

Humboldt State University’s Theatre, Film & Dance Department is proud to announce its participation with the 2008 Kennedy Center American College Theatre Festival; this year’s regional festival will be held in Los Angeles in February 2008.

Started in 1969 by Roger L. Stevens, the Kennedy Center's founding chairman, the Kennedy Center American College Theater Festival (KCACTF) is a national theater program involving 18,000 students from colleges and universities nationwide which has served as a catalyst in improving the quality of college theater in the United States. Through state, regional, and national festivals, KCACTF participants celebrate the creative process, see one another's work, and share experiences and insights within the community of theater artists. The KCACTF honors excellence of overall production and offers student artists individual recognition through awards and scholarships in playwriting, acting, criticism, directing, and design.

This year regional respondents nominated five actors for competition in the Irene Ryan acting awards: Megan Hughes, Eric Rhea, and Lindsey Tewksbury from Urinetown: The Musical and Joseph Castro and Johanna Hembry from Relative Captivity. Many student designers and technicians will also participate in the festival's Design Showcase, Costume Parade, and workshop sessions.

In addition to the student activity, faculty sponsors Rae Robison and Jody Sekas will be offering a series of workshops at the festival on portfolio building and Scenography. Rae Robison serves as the Region’s Circuit 6 Design Coordinator and Costume Parade Director.
