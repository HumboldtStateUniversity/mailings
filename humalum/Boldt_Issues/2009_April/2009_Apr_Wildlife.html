HSU Wildlife Conclave Team Takes First Place Again at the Regional Quiz Bowl!

by Matt Johnson

The HSU Wildlife Conclave team this spring was Leslie Tucci, Chris Smith, Jesse Sargent, and Ryan "Gryphon" Heppler, with alternates Cori Elwood and Dave Spangenburg. The team was coached again by legendary Dave Kitchen, known to conclavers as DK.  The team competed this spring at the Western Regional Wildlife Conclave, at Utah State University in Logan Utah -- we took first again!  This was the first time competing for everyone, except Leslie!  Leslie was captain, and reports are that Chris was one of the strongest players!! They got tricky questions such as how many teeth do true toads have (none, Chris Smith), what do you call a group of owls (a parliament, Leslie), what is the name of the last known Pinta Island (Galapagos) tortoise (lonesome George, Jesse), and what does CITES stand for (a mouthful: Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora, Leslie). 

It was a double elimination tournament, however we never lost a game, playing 8 minute rounds against first Nebraska, then Idaho, and then New Mexico, the other top team there.  We initially beat NM with a score of 85 - 0, and during the 20 minute championship round against them, we got up to 65 points before they scored against us. The final score of the championship match was 155 to 65.

There were also oral presentation, poster, and art competitions.  Freshman David Smith gave his very first talk (!) on Brucellosis  (a wildlife disease) and scored second place and Leslie gave one on California gull research and management in the SF Bay and placed 1st.  Ryan placed 2nd in the poster competition on his ethology class (wldf 475) project involving green-winged teals. Not all the contests are completely academic: The team participated in an animal behavior re-enactment contest, and the they did a skit depicting blood-sharing in vampire bats(!); and there was an animal calling contest, where the team demonstrated their pacific northwest owl hooting skills. They didn't place in either, but had a lot of fun.

Memorable moments... Leslie took everyone to a graveyard known to have white-winged crossbills, which were "lifers" for every student present!  They got great looks at them.  The best part of the trip...  a post-conclave snowball fight with the other teams at our hotel that lasted until really late.  The 1st night at the Welcome mixer was pretty quiet and boring until the DJ put on James Brown and then all the Humboldters got up and showed the other schools how it's done, and even got a few to join in. DK, again, has amazing dance moves. They worked really, really well together as a team, and DK was super supportive and proud of the team, and he was very involved--not just a coach or chaperone but our friend.  Everyone participated one way or another, and helped each other learn things they weren't so strong in--for example, Cori helped us learn a lot of reptile and amphibian stuff, and we were able to answer all the herp questions thanks to her. 

Once again, the wildlife conclave team makes HSU proud!  Their continued success is a testament to the strong wildlife curriculum here at HSU, HSU's tradition of small classes & hands-on learning, the prowess of Dave Kicthen as coach and leader, and -- most of all -- the dedication and drive of our wildlife students!
