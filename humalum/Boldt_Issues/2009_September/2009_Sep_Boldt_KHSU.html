KHSU Begins Golden Anniversary Year

When they started the radio classroom in a corner of Founders Hall in 1941, Humboldt State College professors Don Karshner and John Van Duzer probably couldn’t imagine what they had started. What began in the radio classes of Humboldt State College in the 1940s and 50s and was officially licensed in October 1960, has since become the most listened to radio station in Humboldt County and a voice for a whole community.

KHSU-FM 90.5 begins a year-long celebration of its 50th anniversary on October 24 with a kick-off party at Arcata Theater Lounge. The kick-off event begins a year full of concerts, on-air histories, events and programming planned to celebrate the people and past of KHSU, and its predecessor KHSC. A complete calendar of events is online at www.khsu.org/anniversary.

The party will feature music by Humboldt State University professor Eugene Novotney’s Calypso group, the brass antics of Bandemonium and Cajun-spiced dance music from the Bayou Swamis. For the $10 ticket, party-goers will also get birthday cake and see a screening of the 1995 documentary on KHSU, “AirFare.” In between bands and blowing out candles, photos from more than 50 years of station history and memories from students, volunteers and staff will entertain the crowd. Tickets will be sold at the door of the Arcata Theater Lounge, 1036 G Street Arcata.

The station is also producing specials featuring former volunteers, students and staff to air during the next year. If you were involved in radio classes, KHSC or KHSU, your stories of the early days are needed to build an audio history. Contact Katie Whiteside at the station’s main number 707-826-4807.
