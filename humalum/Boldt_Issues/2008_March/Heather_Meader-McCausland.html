When the moon became our sun….

On March 13 and 14, Heather Meader-McCausland shared her photographs of the interior arctic and spoke about her experiences of living in the wilderness and navigating it’s social and environmental terrain.

Dissatisfied with what was considered "progress" in the late 1950's, Heather's parents moved to an isolated valley in the Brooks Range wilderness of Northern Alaska, 100 miles above the Arctic Circle and 250 miles from the nearest road.  In 2006, Heather along with Ryan Emenaker, a local community organizer and Political Science Instructor at College of the Redwoods, moved back to this isolated valley to live for nearly a year and a half. 

Prior to returning to the Brooks Range, Heather lived in Humboldt Co. for 9 years as an active member of the community and graduated from Humboldt State University in 2005.

Although Heather did not grow up in Humboldt Co. her family ties to Humboldt go back to 1956 when her father taught school in the two-room school house in Petrolia.   In 1957, her brother, Dion, was born in Fortuna.  Petrolia was one of the last communities that her family lived in before deciding to move to an isolated arctic valley where they settled for nearly two decades. Nestled in the Brooks Range Mountains, her family made a life off the land, hunting migrating bands of caribou and making their home, furniture and much of their clothing from the materials of the land.

After her father's and brother's deaths in the mid and late 70's, her mother, Elaine, moved with Heather and her sister Dawn to Northern CA.

Heather moved to Humboldt in 1997.   She and many friends from Humboldt Co. returned to Alaska in 2002 to take care of the land and rebuild the cabins.

In 2006, Heather and Ryan returned to the original homestead living without electricity, generators, and without roads and motorized equipment such as chainsaws and snowmobiles.  They saw about 7 different people in that year and spent much of their time working and learning from the intense arctic environment.
