Department Welcomes Alison Purcell, Ph.D.

The Department of Environmental and Natural Resources Sciences is pleased to welcome Dr. Alison Purcell as the new faculty member and Program Coordinator of the Environmental Science Program in the College of Natural Resources and Sciences.  Dr. Purcell’s background is primarily in ecological restoration/rehabilitation of riparian and freshwater systems.   Her research has focused on post-project assessments of restoration projects in both urban and natural settings in California.  Dr. Purcell also has research interests in aquatic ecology of benthic macroinvertebrates and fish and their use as biological indicators of water quality.  Alison comes to us from UC Berkeley and is enthusiastic about continuing her research in the Humboldt area. 

Dr. Purcell will teach four brand-new courses in Environmental Science: “Introduction to Environmental Science” (ENVS 110), “Environmental Problem Solving” (ENVS 230), “Principles in Ecological Restoration” (ENVS 350), and “Applied Ecological Restoration” (ENVS 450). She hopes to bring an increased feeling of continuity to the Environmental Science Program through the expansion and development of these new Environmental Science courses.

Dr. Purcell is eager to become acquainted with others working in stream and river restoration in the area.  She can be contacted at:
