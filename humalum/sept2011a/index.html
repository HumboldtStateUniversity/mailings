Join HSU For Homecoming & Family Weekend!

It’s less than two weeks until Humboldt State’s Homecoming & Family Weekend. Sept 30 to Oct. 1. If you’ve been thinking about visiting your alma mater, now is the time. There’s lots of fun being planned!

Friday, September 30

10 a.m. to 2 p.m. Campus Tour

Get reacquainted with your campus. Free tours leave every two hours.

4 p.m. Taste of Humboldt Welcome

At Humboldt, you’re right in the middle of some great agriculture, so have a taste on us. Come for a free sample of what our local eateries have to offer.

7 p.m. Women's Volleyball vs. UC San Diego

Saturday, October 1

9 a.m. Humboldt Bay Kayak Tour or Guided Community Forest Walk

By land or by sea, we’ll help you fit some outside time into your Homecoming schedule with a guided tour of the community forest OR a kayak tour of Humboldt Bay.

10 a.m. Classes without Quizzes

Lively lectures with HSU professors. Enjoy the learning and don’t sweat the notes—there will NOT be a test.

2 to 4 p.m. Arts Showcase

Take a tour of art labs, get inspired at the campus galleries, or sneak a peek at the upcoming musical production, Brigadoon.

4 to 6 p.m. Tailgate Party and Food Festival

Join in for good family fun. There will be a ton of kid-friendly activities, food from some of Arcata’s favorite food trucks and appearances by Lucky Logger and the famous Humboldt State Marching Lumberjacks band.

6 p.m. Football vs. Dixie State

8 p.m. Symphonic Band and Jazz Orchestra concert

And if you’re looking for a grand finale to your Homecoming & Family weekend, but you’re not too interested in sports, come to Fulkerson Recital Hall for invigorating symphonies and swinging jazz tunes.

This is just a sample of what’s in store for this year’s Homecoming & Family Weekend. For more information and a full list of events, check out humboldt.edu/homecoming.

Recent Happenings

The Jacks pulled out a last minute field goal for a 38-36 victory over Central Washington in their first home game of the season. The CBS Sports Network was on hand to capture the excitement in front of a national television audience, while thousands of Jacks fans filled the stadium to capacity. All across the county, more than 500 alumni attended over 70 watch parties. Humboldt Alumni wants to give a special shout out to Tara Weaver (’96, Liberal Studies) and family along with Linda Jackson (’06, Psychology) for their feverish distribution of pom poms and blow-up axes at the alumni tent. Your Input is Needed for HSU's Centennial Celebration

HSU will be celebrating its Centennial in 2013, and your input is needed to plan the celebration. There are three ways you can help. 1) HSU needs local volunteers to participate in an advisory group that will meet a few times on campus over the next year. 2) HSU also needs alumni from outside Humboldt County to participate in either a virtual/email based advisory group or 3) be willing to join us for a regional meeting to discuss Centennial concepts if Humboldt Alumni is in your area over the next year. If you’re interested, please let Humboldt Alumni know how you would like to participate by completing the online form. Thank you!
