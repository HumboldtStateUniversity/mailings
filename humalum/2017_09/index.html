‘Great Memories Came Back!’

Alumni and families gathered recently for Forever Humboldt Student Send-Off events, which welcomed new students just before they made their way to campus.

Students had the chance to hear from alumni, and family members connected as they prepared to send their students to college. Tami Snyder was one alum who came out share memories with the new class. “I absolutely loved participating in the student send-off picnic!” says Snyder. “It felt so good to be connected back to my days at HSU. So many great memories came back!”

During Snyder’s time at HSU she sang in the madrigal group, DJ’d at KRFH, edited the Women's Center’s magazine The Matrix, and worked at the Info Desk in University Center.

Thanks to everyone who volunteered to make the events successful!

Geology Students Test Their Skills in the Mountains of Montana

Randy Moory (Geology ‘72) established the Geology Department Opportunities Fund with a recurring annual gift of $6,000. With the support of Randy’s gift, the department created the Geology Field Camp Scholarship, which awarded $800 each to three Geology students who had a chance to go to Montana for this once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Class Notes

Ian Thomas McDonald, 1998 Art, will soon celebrate his 10 year wedding anniversary with HSU alumna,Natalie Covert, 1997 Art. The couple’s daughter, Haddie, is turning 4 years old, and McDonald is starting a new job running the Ceramics department at the Cranbrook Academy of Art in Michigan.

Daniel C. Hernandez, 1984 Recreation Administration, recently announced his retirement from the field of parks, recreation, and leisure services. For the past six years, he was director of Parks, Recreation, and Community Services for Culver City, Calif. After completing his bachelor’s degree, Hernandez began his full-time career with the City of Santa Barbara Parks and Recreation Department in 1985, overseeing youth sports and special events. In 1996, while serving as Director of Community Services for the City of Carpinteria, he completed his Master’s of Public Administration at CSU Northridge. Hernandez has been a presenter and speaker at both the state and national level.
