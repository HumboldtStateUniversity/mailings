Help HSU Win ‘Outdoorsiest’ Title Again

Crowned the nation’s outdoor champs last year, Humboldt State is defending the title in the fourth annual Outdoor Nation Campus Challenge, which runs through Oct. 15. The contest, designed to get people outside and active, is part of a larger effort to cultivate a new generation of outdoor enthusiasts and stewards of the environment.

Humboldt State is one of nearly 100 colleges and universities across the United States participating in this year’s challenge.

Everyone connected to HSU can participate to earn points. That means alumni as well! The school to log the most outdoor activity will be named “National Outdoor Champion” and receive a gear bank worth $3,000 from North Face.

To join in, just download the Outdoor Nation app (available at the App Store or Google Play) or register on the website

Learn more at hsuoutdoors.humboldt.edu.

Class Notes

Robert "Robin" Huber, 1971 Theatre Design M.A., went on to earn a Ph.D. in Theatre History from the University of Southern California. He is recognized as the foremost authority on The Merced (1870), Los Angeles' first permanent professional theater. Huber retired simultaneously from both the California State University and Community College systems in 2009. He has taught at CSULA, Dominguez Hills, Golden West, and Cerritos colleges. While at Humboldt he was "adopted" by Marine Biology professor Gary Brusca, his family, and fellow professors. He’s now embracing that Humboldt natural sciences spirit by working as a volunteer in natural resources at Chino Hills State Park near his home in Yorba Linda.

Mildred J. Correa, 2017 Art, has been offered and accepted an incredible full-time career opportunity at a nonprofit in her hometown, Armory Center for the Arts, Pasadena, Calif. Correa's prior positions during her time at HSU--including at the Academic and Career Advising Center, the Library, the Learning Center, and Student Access Gallery--guided her to realize that she values working for organizations that positively impact community through design. Correa is happily employed at the Armory where everyday she is spreading awareness about this education and gallery space.
