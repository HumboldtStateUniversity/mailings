Latest Class Notes

Garrett John Costello (‘14, Environmental Science & Management) During the last two years, Costello and his wife (an HSU alumna) got married and started an environmental consulting business. As their business has grown they have turned their attention to constructing low-tech beaver dam analogs to help restore critical stream and meadow ecosystems. This year Symbiotic Restoration installed more than100 beaver dams across Northern California. As their business expands they hope to be able to employ and incorporate Humboldt students into these programs. Their current plans include looking for land so that they can move back to Humboldt County and build their home back where their journey began.

Pamela Hartvig Range (‘67, Social Work) Practicing psychotherapist, expressive arts therapist, and artist, she went on to get her MSW at CSUS, an MFA in paint from California College of Arts and Crafts, and Ph.D. in Expressive Therapy. She is practicing privately in Sacramento, California, and has worked as a medical SW and all types of out-patient mental health and clinical supervision. She has shown her art—oil paintings and prints—all over, has done abstract landscapes for 30 years, and has switched to the human form.

Lucas Allen-Custodio, (‘18, Zoology) She acquired a job as a laboratory technician at a local soil lab within two months of graduating. She is now the lead lab tech and is using her Zoology degree as the agricultural pest management advisor in the company. Through this, she specializes in providing the agricultural industry with environmentally conscious biological control remedies that reduce the use of pesticides, as well as provide technical information to farmers on how to take care of and love their soil, based on laboratory results.
