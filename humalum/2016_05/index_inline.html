Alumni Artists Open their Studios

Three HSU alumni are among the featured artists in the 8th annual North Coast Open Studios artist’s tour, taking place June 4‐5 and 11‐12 throughout Humboldt County.

John Wesa ('91, Art), Patricia Sennott ('85, Art), and Julie Sessa (FW note ‐ any link for Julie?) ('81, Business) will open their studios to the public for this free event.

All three artists draw a direct connection between their time at HSU and their current work.

"My art has always been nature based and of course, we have such a beautiful campus. I actually took a lot of science classes not just art and that influenced my art. I use to go into the greenhouse to sketch, and today there are still botanical references in my work,” says Sennott, who produces nature‐themed prints and watercolors in her Arcata studio.

Learn more about North Coast Open Studios at 2015.northcoastopenstudios.com

Experience HSU Again—This Time as Our Guest!

Humboldt State University is opening up it residence halls for alumni this summer.

Choose from a variety of accommodations to meet your needs. Suites have up to six bedrooms and feature a living room and full kitchen. Rates start at $37 per night (one to two people) for residence hall housing, $100 per night (up to ten people) for suites, and $150 per night (four people) in a beautiful apartment.

You must have a Forever Humboldt Alumni ID to qualify for this special Alumni Stay program.

Latest Class Notes

Robert P. Parker, 2011 Fisheries Biology, began working for the Nebraska Department of Environmental Quality as a Clean Water Act Section 401 coordinator in March.

Jeremy Bisson, 1999 Wildlife, spent six years working as a biologist in the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration Fisheries Pacific Islands Regional Office Observer Program after graduating HSU. In 2006, Bisson moved to south‐central Idaho, where he has been working as a wildlife biologist in the Burley Bureau of Land Management Field Office for the last nine years. His focus in Idaho has been sage‐grouse habitat restoration where he’s had success in developing a landscape‐scale habitat restoration plan and collaboration.
