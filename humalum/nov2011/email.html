Alum Searches for Meaning As Population Hits 7 Billion

Alum Kaitlin Yarnall (‘05 Geography) is part of team of National Geographic Society researchers investigating population. Experts at the United Nations say global population hit 7 billion on October 31. Kaitlin’s work was recently featured on CBS News in a thought-provoking piece on what it means to be the most typical human in a crowd of billions.

HSU Seniors Awarded Alumni Association Scholarships

HSU students Courtney Snyder and Samuel Sellers are the recipients the spring 2012 Alumni Association Scholarship. Every semester Humboldt Alumni awards a $1,000 scholarship to two current HSU students who are related to an alum. It’s not too early to apply for next year. Visit this link for more information (PDF) »

While Sam Sellers is deeply interested in his major, Computer Information Systems, he looks forward to a career in law enforcement, having graduated at the top of his class in the 109th Police Academy at College of the Redwoods. After earning his degree, Sam hopes to further his law enforcement training and to eventually join the San Diego Drug Task Force in patrolling the California-Mexico border. Sam follows in his mother's footsteps in becoming an HSU alum.

Courtney Snyder is an Anthropology major focusing on cross-cultural birthing techniques. In 2010, she studied abroad at the Xi’an International Studies University in China, which helped her earn a minor in Chinese Studies. After graduating, Courtney hopes to become a certified midwife along with earning certification in other areas of perinatal care. Courtney's older sister is an HSU alum.

A very special thanks is due to the members of the scholarship committee who helped select this semester’s winners from among the many submitted applications. Thanks again!
