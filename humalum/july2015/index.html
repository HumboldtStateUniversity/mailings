Alumni Gather for Prof’s Retirement Concert

Former students of Department of Music Professor Harley Muilenburg performed for his retirement concert on June 27. Over the years Muilenburg, who began teaching at HSU in 1983, transformed the small HSU chamber choir into the large concert choir now called the University Singers. He also started Mad River Transit (MRT) Singers as a smaller adjunct to the existing jazz ensemble, eventually combining the two in today’s slightly larger MRT.

Bragging About HSU

HSU is Tops for Nature Lovers

Humboldt State University has been named the No. 1 college for nature lovers by the website Great Value Colleges, an online higher educational resource database. In rating HSU, the authors cited Humboldt’s curriculum and unique location near redwood forests, beaches, and mountains. Of course, none of this is news to our alumni!

Kenneth Davis, 2001, Film Production (MA), founded Pro Cuts Editing Services, a postproduction firm in the Baltimore/Washington area in 2002, after earning a master’s degree in Film Production. The firm provides services to videographers, corporate and government clients, independent filmmakers, and media enthusiasts. Davis remains active with SCUBA and adores his new niece, Krista.

Joshua Martin (formerly known as Joshua Roberts) 1998, English & History,, worked at the Hotel Arcata for about a year after graduating. Martin then entered law school at the University of Utah, accompanied by his girlfriend, Bonnie Jo McLauchlan (also an HSU graduate). Martin made law review, graduated, and practiced criminal defense in Salt Lake City. He later moved on to civil litigation. In 2007 Martin moved to San Louis Obispo County and opened the Law Offices of Joshua W. Martin in 2013. Martin married Bonnie Jo and the couple lives in Grover Beach, Calif., with their two boys, Max, 10, and Charlie, 5, and their dog, Scooby.

Max Brunsfeld, 2011, Physics & Astronomy, moved to Oakland after graduating from HSU and worked as a software engineer in San Francisco for three years. Brunsfeld recently started working for GitHub, a company that makes tools for collaborative software development, and whose employees are distributed all over the world. Brunsfeld has since moved back to Arcata to raise a family.
