Julia Hostler

Julia Hostler just finished her first semester at Humboldt State University after transferring from College of the Redwoods. Previously, she earned an Associate of Science degree from Haskell Indian Nations University in Kansas. She is a single parent of four and a member of the Hoopa Valley Tribe. Julia is majoring in Native American Studies with Business Administration minor. She plans to serve her community by pursuing a leadership role with the Hoopa Valley Tribe. Julia currently is a board member for the Hoopa Valley Public Utilities District and has volunteered with several youth organizations. She is proud to set a strong example of academic achievement for her four children.

Anna Schneider

Anna Schneider is a junior earning a bachelor’s degree in Studio Art. She holds herself to high academic standards, even while working two jobs. Her work with Humboldt Educare Preschool has inspired her to pursue an associate preschool teacher’s permit through HSU’s Child Development Department. On campus, Anna is involved with Planned Parenthood’s VOX club and the HSU Photo Club, having participated in numerous events for both organizations. Her long-term plans include building a strong portfolio of her artwork and continuing her education with a Master of Fine Arts in Photography.
