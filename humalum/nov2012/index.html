Mapping Alumni

Where in the world do HSU graduates end up? Using the current locations 50,000+ alumni, Geography major Stephen Mangum set out to help illustrate the answer. He created a series of maps—one for each decade starting in the 1950s—showing where non-California alumni now live.

Recent Happenings

More than 60 alumni got together recently for a private tour and tasting at Chateau Ste. Michelle Winery in Washington state. Alumni had a great time socializing and reminiscing about HSU, sharing stories, enjoying good wine and the gorgeous setting. And a few alumni truly enjoyed winning the drawings for Humboldt Made products.

See more photos from this event on the Alumni Facebook page.

HSU Forestry alums recently attended the Society of American Foresters National Convention in Spokane, Wash.

Part of the convention included alumni receptions, which are great ways to catch up, network and share memories with fellow professional foresters. More than 100 people attended the SAF Convention’s joint alumni reception, held in partnership with UC Berkeley and Cal Poly San Luis Obispo.

Thank You Alumni Volunteers!

Humboldt Alumni & HSU’s Office of Admissions would like to thank alumni Lynn Huynh (’11 Theater Arts), Tiffany Newton (’08 Women’s Studies and Journalism), Julia West (’07 Economics), and Mitch Waldow, (’76 Journalism) for their support in Southern California. They represented HSU at various college fairs, speaking with prospective students about their experience and why they chose HSU.

We are truly thankful and honored to have you represent Humboldt State University!

Another big thanks is due to Gretchen Newsom (’04 Political Science), for her volunteer efforts supporting the Jacks on the road at the Azusa Pacific Football Tailgate event. HSU’s 800+ fans made it feel almost like a home game!

Snapshot From the 60s

Alumnus Bill Stankus (’69, Psychology) contacted the Alumni Office recently to share some photos from his time on campus.

Bill had some great photos in the mix, including bed races from the 1967 Lumberjack Days and photos of North Town Arcata. It’s fun trying to identify all the buildings and streets in these historic photos.

Is there a shoebox in your closet filled with photos from your HSU days? You should share them! Submit your photos and stories at humboldt.edu/centennial/submit.

Thanks Bill!
