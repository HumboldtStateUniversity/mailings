Volunteer Opportunity

Become an Alumni Mentor

HSU students are part of a network of more than 60,000 alumni. As an Alumni Mentor, you can give back to this community by sharing your experiences and advice about life after graduation. It’s a great opportunity to share career guidance and insights on personal development. Students benefit from the contact with professionals in the current working world.

You'll not only enjoy a rewarding volunteer experience and stay connected to the HSU community, you'll help further the development of today's students.

Alumni Mentors manage their own account and profiles online, allowing you to personalize your involvement in a way that suits you. Learn more and sign up!

Latest Class Notes

Melody Stone, 2007 Journalism, after three years in public media at Capital Public Radio, just started as the marketing and communication specialist at the University of Nevada, Reno journalism school. Stone is also teaching a design lab.

Taylor Kilgore, 2011 Communication, moved to Colorado after accepting a sports anchor position at CBS KKTV. Kilgore now gets to cover teams like the Broncos, Rockies, Nuggets, and the Air Force Academy.

Mike Margolies, 1976 Kinesiology & Recreation Administration, moved to Dallas Texas in 2015 to work with Mental Training, Inc., and in 2016 he was appointed chief operating officer of the company. The company trains athletes, performing artists, and executives around the world to be mentally tough. Margolies writes, “I owe my entire career start to the education I received at HSU.”
