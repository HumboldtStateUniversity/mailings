HSU Honors Distinguished Alumni

What do this year's Distinguished Alumni all have in common? Besides their HSU connection, they all exemplify excellence in their fields. This year's recipients include the president of North Dakota State University, the head of an engineering firm, a leading medical researcher, and a MacArthur Foundation Genius Grant winner. Dean Bresciani, Kenneth Davlin, Chester Mathis, and Marla Spivak are the 2012 Distinguished Alumni. Congratulations!

DEAN BRESCIANI – (‘84, Sociology) After realizing his passion for higher education at Humboldt State, Bresciani has held several top posts at universities across the country. He is currently the 14th President of North Dakota State University.

KENNETH DAVLIN – Davlin studied engineering at HSU from 1959-1962 and was even a member of the Marching Lumberjacks and named Physics Student of the Year. He continues to support the school as a member of the University Advancement Foundation board. Davlin is President of Oscar Larson & Associates, a California-based engineering consulting firm.

CHESTER MATHIS – (‘72, Chemistry) A former HSU valedictorian, Mathis is currently an Endowed Chair Professor of Radiology and Pharmaceutical Sciences and Director of the Positron Emission Tomography Facility at the University of Pittsburgh. His innovative work has made advances in the understanding of Alzheimer's disease and he has recently begun working in similar areas on Parkinson's disease.

MARLA SPIVAK – (‘78, Biological Sciences) Currently the Distinguished McKnight Professor in Entomology at the University of Minnesota, this internationally renowned honeybee health expert received a MacArthur Foundation Genius Grant in 2010. Spivak has used her award to establish the Bee Squad, an organization aimed at educating the public about the importance of honeybees in the Twin Cities area.
