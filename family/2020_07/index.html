July 2020

Helping your Student Succeed
this Fall

Enoch Hale, the Director of the Center for Teaching & Learning (CTL) at HSU has offered some ways your student can be in a position to succeed this fall semester:

HSU takes teaching and learning excellence seriously, and wants you to know that your students will have access to all the resources they will need to have a successful learning experience whether on campus or virtually. To this end, the CTL has been working hard to support HSU students in preparation for the Fall 2020 semester and beyond. Some of the things that the CTL has been working on include:

  • Providing robust professional development and support for all HSU instructors so they can continue to offer the highest quality of education regardless of where and how learning takes place.
  • Developing a comprehensive one-stop website for students that links them to everything from tutoring and advising to how-to guides for navigating Canvas and other technologies like Zoom.
  • Developing a resource in Canvas that helps them organize their academic responsibilities as well as providing guidance on the most productive methods for studying.

Here is how you can help:

  • Please encourage your student to regularly visit the Keep Learning website for updates and links to various campus resources.
  • These are challenging times, but please remember your student is not alone. There are many people at HSU who are here to help, so encourage them to reach out and connect with faculty and advisors.

HSU Campus Ready Website is Live

Humboldt State University is developing and refining plans for instruction, flexible repopulation of campus, and the student experience during the 2020-21 academic year.

The most comprehensive information about the ongoing operations is available at campusready.humboldt.edu.

There, you can see information specific to students, as well as information on a variety of topics affecting the entire campus community. The website will continue to be updated as the University prepares and responds to the pandemic.
