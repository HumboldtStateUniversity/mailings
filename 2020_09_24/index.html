A Message from Humboldt State University

August 17, 2020

Strategic Planning Process Kicks Off

Last spring, members of the campus community began creating Humboldt State’s Strategic Plan (2021-2026). Essential to the improvement and growth of HSU, this plan will articulate who we are as an institution now and who we want to be in five years.

The planning process, which had been postponed due to the pandemic, recently relaunched with a renewed sense of energy and purpose to develop a bold vision that’s transformational and, at the same time, adaptable to the new reality of higher education.

Leading the process is President Jackson’s Strategic Initiatives Committee, which develops, implements, and monitors strategic and operational planning. The committee is supported by the Technical Team and the Administrative Support Team. For details, go to the Strategic Plan website.

These groups are working to meet the President’s charge:

“In order to provide a positive and meaningful educational experience for all HSU students, the steering committee and working groups should seek to establish a model global community and to integrate diversity, equity, and inclusion within its planning. The committee should envision HSU as a bold, innovative institution that embraces and celebrates being a Minority Serving Institution and Hispanic Serving Institution.”

The Strategic Plan will be informed by findings and recommendations from WASC Senior College and University Commission and other foundational documents. The process will be guided by a framework of five guiding principles that reflect the committee’s shared commitment to diversity, equity, and inclusion and the University’s status as a Hispanic Serving Institution:

  • Students First: A commitment to a planning process that is driven by the interests of our students, their perspectives on what their current and future success means, and informed by research.
  • Inclusive Process: An inclusive and collaborative process.
  • Future-Focused and Aspirational: A reflection of HSU’s history, a full understanding of the University’s unique assets and challenges while recognizing the necessity for the institution to be forward-thinking, nimble, and action-oriented.
  • Strategic and Focused: HSU’s strategic direction and distinction in the CSU.

Key to designing the Strategic Plan is collaborative engagement. Starting in October, there will be many opportunities for the campus, alumni, donors, and members of the local community to provide feedback on the Strategic Plan (Bold Vision, Values and Strategic Goals). Input and ideas will help the committee refine their work. By December, the committee is expected to present a draft plan to the Senate, which will review the plan and send it to President Jackson. A final draft is also expected by December.

More details on forums, plus updates and information will be communicated in campus-wide messages and posted on the Strategic Plan website.

Thank you for all that you’re doing to shape the future and enhance the student experience.
