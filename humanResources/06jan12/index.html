Social Security Taxes

The law to extend the Social Security tax cut was signed on December 23, 2011; this occurred after the State Controller’s Office issued the December pay period warrants. Therefore, employees that are subject to Social Security taxes may have noticed a change in the tax amount on your most recent pay warrant. The State Controller’s Office will issue a refund of any overpayment of Social Security taxes no later than February 29, 2012.

Health Benefit Issues

It has come to the attention of Human Resources that some inaccuracies in health premium deductions have occurred in paychecks for employees due to data errors between CalPers and the State Controller’s Office. This may affect employees who have made health plan changes, both adding or deleting dependents and changes in health plan carriers. This may also affect any Open Enrollment changes for January 2012.

If you have made a change of any kind to your health plan since August 2011, please review your December 30, 2011 pay warrant stub to ensure the premiums are accurate as reflected in the information below. Incorrect deductions could result in multiple deductions being owed by you.

NOTE: We are especially concerned if you made changes to, or enrolled in, Blue Shield HMO as premiums are significantly higher. Inaccuracies could result in substantial premiums owed.
