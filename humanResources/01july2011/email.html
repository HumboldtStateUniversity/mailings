FOUR COLLECTIVE BARGAINING AGREEMENTS EXPIRE (APC, CSUEU, SUPA, UAPD)

The Collective Bargaining Agreements between The California State University and the Academic Professionals of California (Unit 4), the California State University Employees Union (Units 2, 5, 7, 9), the Statewide University Police Association (Unit 8), and the California Federation of the Union of American Physicians and Dentists (Unit 1) expired on June 30, 2011. In general, the terms and conditions of the expired collective bargaining agreement continue until a new agreement has been reached or the conclusion of the collective bargaining process.

There are two exceptions to this general rule:

  1. In the absence of a collective bargaining agreement, the federal Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) requires that all overtime worked by non-exempt employees be compensated in cash. Overtime cannot be compensated with CTO (Compensatory Time Off).
  2. The arbitration provision of the collective bargaining agreements does not survive the expiration of the agreement. Therefore, grievances dealing with events that arise after June 30, 2011 will not be subject to arbitration.
