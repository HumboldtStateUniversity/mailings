A Message from Humboldt State University

March 1, 2021

Face-to-face Courses Begin March 8

Dear Lumberjacks,

We’d like to welcome you back from Spring Break and thank you for your ongoing commitment to keeping yourself and the campus community safe and healthy.

Move-in took place recently for those students who weren’t already living on campus, and we are excited for limited face-to-face courses to resume on Monday, March 8.

It’s an important time to stay engaged. We encourage you to connect with your faculty and your classmates, reach out if you need support in any way, and know the resources available to ensure your success. We also urge you to revisit your Social Responsibility Commitment to the health and safety of yourself, the campus, and community.

We’ve launched a Basic Needs website, where you can find campus and community resources on food, housing, mental health, and child care resources, and more.

HSU received $16 million in the latest round of funding to support the University during the pandemic, with at least $5 million earmarked directly for emergency financial aid grants to students. More than 4,100 students have received notices that they automatically were awarded grant funds, and those funds should be deposited into student accounts soon.

Additional funding is available for students who didn’t automatically qualify. Request forms are now available. To request aid, visit the Financial Aid website.

Free COVID-19 testing for all students is still available this semester. You can sign up for an appointment on the Student Health Center Website. Virtual Counseling & Psychological Services are also free and available to students.

HSU is strongly recommending that students get COVID-19 vaccines as soon as possible. Humboldt County residents can submit their contact information through an online form to be notified when doses are available for their tier. Fill out the COVID-19 Vaccine Interest form and read more about the COVID-19 vaccine from the CDC.

We understand that changes to this Spring’s academic calendar caused some frustration, and we’re sorry for the inconvenience this has caused. The changes were made in response to new State and CSU guidelines responding to the ever-changing environment of the COVID-19 pandemic.

Your flexibility as we plan, prepare, and respond to the pandemic has been crucial to our success, and we appreciate your support, patience, and feedback. Plans are underway for the Fall semester, which you can read about here. We will release more details as they are available.

And, as always, please remember to take the necessary precautions for the safety of our campus and community. Wear a face covering, maintain social distance, conduct wellness self-checks before visiting campus, and keep in mind your social responsibility commitment.

Stay healthy and have a great semester,

Jenn Capps, Provost
Eboni Turnbow, Dean of Students
