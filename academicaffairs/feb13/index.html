Here at Humboldt State improving student success is a priority. Today I’d like to announce a new scheduling system that will help us toward that goal.

A recent survey of students indicated that one challenge they face that has inhibited their success is the availability of needed classes. Nearly one-quarter of students reported difficulty enrolling in courses required for graduation. So last fall, the campus established a Scheduling Working Group to examine issues around course scheduling and to identify ways to improve our course scheduling so students can take the classes they need.

The Working Group, which included faculty, staff, students and administrators, looked at scheduling practices at a number of other institutions and solicited input from across campus. As a result of this effort, the group has developed a series of new scheduling rules to be put into place for Fall 2013.

To summarize the changes, the schedule will be split into a series of two-hour blocks. Nearly all courses will now begin on the hour. Within a department, courses will be distributed evenly among blocks and days. This is intended to reduce conflicts for students by reducing the number of classes in a department that are held concurrently, and giving students a wider range of times during which relevant classes will be offered. Administrative meetings will also be subject to the scheduling rules, again to minimize conflicts.

There are a number of other changes to allow for some flexibility and the needs of unique courses. I encourage you all to review the Scheduling Working Group’s report, available here:

http://bit.ly/XUfSPV

I want to personally thank the members of the Scheduling Working Group, who have undertaken an enormous challenge and delivered valuable solutions in a short time frame, and who will continue to meet to evaluate this new system.

While these changes will demand some flexibility from faculty in scheduling their courses, I believe that these changes will put our students in a better position to succeed.

Robert Snyder
Provost & Vice President for Academic Affairs
