Turn a tax burden into a gift to Humboldt State.

If you have a retirement plan, such as a traditional IRA or 401(k) and you want it to benefit your children some day, you may be surprised to learn that the IRS and many states will impose income tax on the account balance if you designate it to a beneficiary other than your spouse.

Depending on the size of your estate, your plan might be further reduced by estate tax. The result: up to 70% of the value of your retirement assets could be consumed in taxes before your loved ones receive it.

There’s another way to make the most of your assets: Name the HSU Advancement Foundation as the beneficiary, or partial beneficiary, of your retirement plan.

Then use other assets not subject to income tax — such as appreciated stock or the family home ­­— to make gifts to your heirs. The HSU Advancement Foundation will receive the remaining plan value tax-free, and your heirs may enjoy more of your generosity.

Your kids win, and so do ours.
