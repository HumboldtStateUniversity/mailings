Dear Campus Community,

I hope you will join an important discussion about increasing private philanthropic support for Humboldt State University. Our discussion will be informed by the consulting firm Bentz Whaley Flessner.

You are invited to participate by attending an open forum from noon to 2 p.m., Thursday, April 1 in the University Center Banquet Room.

Over the last few years, Humboldt State University has made significant progress in attracting philanthropic support. In fact, each of the last three fiscal years has been at, or very near, the top three years ever in the number of alumni donors, the number of all donors, and total giving excluding bequests. HSU is on pace to set new records in each of those areas again this fiscal year. Given that we are entering our third year of a deep economic recession, this is particularly strong evidence of the appreciation HSU alumni and supporters have for our faculty, coaches, staff, students, and programs.

It is from this newfound position of stability and growth in philanthropic support that we engaged the consulting firm of Bentz Whaley Flessner. They have studied the readiness and capacity of the campus community, current and potential supporters of HSU, and the Development Office to increase philanthropic support for the University. Many of you were interviewed, participated in small group discussions, or completed the online survey for the study. Thank you for investing your time in this effort and giving the consultants your thoughts.

Dr. Jim Daniels of Bentz Whaley Flessner will be on campus April 1 to share the findings and lead discussions about the Philanthropic Capacity Assessment Report.

I look forward to listening to the discussion. You can also share your thoughts directly with me or Dr. Laura Jackson, Associate Vice President for Development and Alumni Relations.

We will be utilizing the report and campus input to inform our annual planning process in May and June. In the fall, we anticipate providing a set of strategies for sustaining and expanding philanthropic support. The success of these efforts depends on a broad commitment from all those who care about Humboldt State University, so we will continue to seek and appreciate your input and involvement. I and the entire Advancement team look forward to working with you to continue building philanthropic support for Humboldt State University.

Thank You,

Robert Gunsalus
