A Message from Humboldt State University

July 30, 2018

Loss of HSU Alumna McKenzie Gilbert

Dear University Community:

We are sad to share news of the loss of Humboldt State University recent graduate McKenzie Gilbert.

McKenzie, 23, of Santa Cruz, California died Friday, July 20 in Santa Cruz. A Biology major pursuing a career in health sciences, she had graduated in May. She also devoted her time working in a local hospital and conducting undergraduate research. McKenzie is remembered as a sincere and compassionate person who was determined to make a difference in the world.

We extend our condolences to her family and friends. Her death is a loss for our community, and our thoughts are with all of those who were close to her.

University staff has reached out to provide support to students, faculty, and staff who may have known her.

While Counseling & Psychological Services (CAPS) is closed for in-person services during the summer, students in need of additional support at this time can call CAPS counselors at 707.826.3236. CAPS in-person services resume on Monday, August 20. In the meantime, the Humboldt Family Service Center (HFSC), offers a free walk-in clinic each Monday from 1 to 4 pm. HFSC, located at 1802 California St. in Eureka, can be reached at 707.443.7358. Students may also contact the Dean of Students office at 211 Siemens Hall or 707.826.3504. Staff or faculty seeking additional support may call the Employee Assistance Program at 707.443.7358. If you are in crisis and need immediate assistance, please call the local crisis line at 707.445.7715.
