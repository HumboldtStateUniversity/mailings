HSU Swine Flu Advisory

HSU officials and the campus Student Health Center are closely monitoring the human swine flu outbreak and will provide regular updates about the campus response at www.humboldt.edu and at www.humboldt.edu/~emergency.

The campus community is encouraged to visit the federal Centers for Disease Control and Prevention in Atlanta for the latest national advisories at www.cdc.gov/swineflu.

Influenza, including swine flu, spreads mainly person-to-person from the coughing and sneezing of infected people. There are multiple kinds of flu with similar symptoms. A number are active locally, including common cold and flu-like illnesses that are not swine flu. Symptoms may mimic one another from one infection to the next.

This particular swine flu concerns public health officials because it is not prevented by the most recent flu shot, meaning it could spread rapidly.

Suggested precautions:

  • Stay three to six feet away from those you observe coughing or sneezing. Avoid shaking hands.
  • Wash your hands thoroughly and often with soap and hot water, especially after you sneeze or cough.
  • When soap and hot water are not readily available, alcohol-based hand wipes are effective.
  • Avoid touching your eyes, nose or mouth to prevent the spread of germs.
  • If you feel ill, CDC recommends you stay home from school or work and limit contact with others as much as possible to prevent infecting them.

A campus plan for dealing with the outbreak is in place and the University is proceeding with preparedness measures. The Student Health Center is equipped with an isolation facility to help prevent the spread of infection. It can also purchase anti-viral drugs, but these would be dispensed only to those diagnosed with swine flu.

Campus community members who are feeling unwell are advised to stay at home and get plenty of rest. Symptoms include a temperature of 100 degrees or higher, sore throat, runny nose, nasal congestion, coughing spells and general flu symptoms like headache and body aches. Gastrointestinal symptoms may include nausea, vomiting and diarrhea.

Students should report to the Student Health Center if they are experiencing shortness of breath or unable to keep liquids down due to vomiting.

At this time, CDC recommends that U.S. travelers avoid all nonessential travel to Mexico.

Campus updates are available at Humboldt State at www.humboldt.edu and www.humboldt.edu/emergency.

Comprehensive information is available at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention at www.humboldt.edu/emergency, www.cdc.gov/swineflu and the World Health Organization at http://www.who.int/csr/disease/swineflu/en/index.html.
