Dear HSU Students:

Many of you will be returning this weekend from the spring break. As you know, Humboldt County often experiences significant storms at this time of year. The roadways can be hazardous due to rain, hail, sleet, black ice and/or snow. Please use caution in your travels and check on road conditions before your departure from, or return to, the area. Our local highways are U.S. Highway 101, Sate Route 199, State Route 299 and State Route 36. Additionally, Interstate 5 is the main highway for north-south travel throughout the western states.

Here are some resources to check the current conditions before travel.

Caltrans road info:
800-427-7623 (800-GAS-ROAD) or http://www.dot.ca.gov/cgi-bin/roads.cgi

CA state weather reports: http://www.weather.gov/view/states.php?state=ca&map=on

Watches, Warnings or Advisories for CA:
http://www.weather.gov/alerts-beta/ca.php?x=1

Watches, Warnings or Advisories for the North Coast zone (CAZ201):
http://www.weather.gov/alerts-beta/wwaatmget.php?x=CAZ201

Our concern is for your safety; by driving appropriately for the weather and road conditions, and being aware of driver fatigue, you should arrive at your destination safely.

We look forward to your return to campus for instruction on Monday, March 21.

Take good care,

Randi Darnall Burke
Dean of Students
