Dear HSU Students:

Many of you will be leaving soon for the Thanksgiving break. Please be aware that Humboldt County often experiences significant storms at this time of year. The roadways can be hazardous due to rain, hail, sleet, black ice, and/or snow. Please use caution in your travels during the break and check on road conditions before your departure from or return to the area. Our local highways are 101, 199, 299 and 36. Additionally, Interstate 5 is the main highway for north-south travel throughout the western states.

Here are some resources to check the current conditions before travel.

Caltrans road info:
800-427-7623 (800-GAS-ROAD) or http://www.dot.ca.gov/cgi-bin/roads.cgi

CA state weather reports:
http://www.weather.gov/view/states.php?state=ca&map=on

Watches, Warnings or Advisories for CA:
http://www.weather.gov/alerts-beta/ca.php?x=1

Watches, Warnings or Advisories for the North Coast zone (CAZ201):
http://www.weather.gov/alerts-beta/wwaatmget.php?x=CAZ201

Our concern is for your safety; by using common sense, driving appropriately for the weather and road conditions, and being aware of driver fatigue, you should arrive at your destination safely.

We look forward to your return to campus for instruction on Monday, November 28, 2011.

Take good care,

Peg L. Blake
Vice President, Enrollment Management and Student Affairs
