Dear HSU Community,

Sexualized violence is something that affects us all. The National Institute of Justice reports that one in five women are victims of sexual assault while enrolled in college, and just over six percent of men are. Acts of sexualized violence are motivated by sexism, racism, transphobia, homophobia, ableism, xenophobia, classism and/or ageism.

Humboldt State University is proud of our student community and of our students' commitment to social justice. We thank our students, faculty, staff and other allies for their work and encourage you to become further engaged in learning how we can work toward ending sexualized violence. One way to do that is by participating in Take Back The Night 2011, happening April 11-15. (http://www.humboldt.edu/hsuwomen/eventsCalendar.html).

We look forward to your participation in building awareness about and preventing sexualized violence.

The full schedule, along with descriptions of each event is available on the Women's Resource Center's website event page: http://www.humboldt.edu/hsuwomen/eventsCalendar.html

Sincerely,
