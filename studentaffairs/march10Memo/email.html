Dear HSU Students:

It's time to enjoy Spring Break; our concern is for your safety. Many of you will be traveling. If driving, please remember that bad weather, mountainous roads, driving under the influence, and/or driver fatigue too often leads to vehicular accidents and tragedy.

Highway 199 between Crescent City and Grants Pass was closed yesterday due to a large rock slide; today it is reopened. Please use caution in your travels during the break and check on road conditions before your departure from and return to the area.

Here are some resources to check regarding current conditions before travel.

  • Caltrans road info: 800-427-7623 (800-GAS-ROAD) or
    http://www.dot.ca.gov/cgi-bin/roads.cgi
  • CA state weather reports:
    http://www.weather.gov/view/states.php?state=ca&map=on
  • Watches, Warnings or Advisories for CA:
    http://www.weather.gov/alerts-beta/ca.php?x=1
  • Watches, Warnings or Advisories for the North Coast zone (CAZ201):
    http://www.weather.gov/wwarss-tst/wwaatmget.php?x=CAZ001

If you are fortunate enough to be planning international travel during the break, including Mexico, please check the U.S. State Department website for warnings and alerts @ http://travel.state.gov/travel/cis_pa_tw/cis_pa_tw_1168.html

Lastly, the Center for Disease Control (CDC) and national student health organizations recommend that you be immunized for the H1N1 flu prior to traveling, as the disease is on the upswing nationally and internationally. Immunizations are available at the HSU Student Health Center.

Take good care,

Steven Butler
Vice President of Student Affairs
