Dear Campus Community:

The wonderful North Coast fall weather is here and many of you may be heading to the local rivers or beaches this Labor Day weekend. Unfortunately, it seems that every year we see the news stories about someone drowning in the river or at the beach here on the North Coast. Here is some basic safety information so that you can enjoy our beautiful beaches and rivers with confidence:

  1. The rivers of Northwestern California can have cold, swift moving water almost any time of year because of snowmelt and irregular reservoir releases. River bottoms often have unexpected drop-offs and slippery rocks which in tandem with swift currents can get people in trouble who are just wading in the river. The rivers also change from year to year – so you can't assume an area that was safe last year is also safe this year. The cold water is a particular problem because it can make your arms and legs not work properly – through a process called cold water paralysis - which makes it difficult to keep your head above water. Your best protection is to always wear a life vest when on the river to help you keep your head above water in case you fall in. You should also check the river for swift currents and steep drop-offs before going in—don't be fooled by a calm appearance on the surface. Sometimes getting a higher vantage point can help you see the true nature of the river.
  2. The North Coast's ocean beaches also have their dangers. Large waves can surprise you almost any time of the year, catching you off guard and pulling you quickly into the ocean. Survival in the surf in this area is difficult because of the strong currents, turbulent surf, and very cold water. The key is to not get pulled into the ocean unexpectedly in the first place. So don't be fooled by waves that look small. They can be small for up to twenty minutes before a big set of waves comes in and surprises you. The best approach is to stay further back from the water than you might think is necessary - definitely way above the wet sand line - and never turn your back on the ocean.

If you have to be close to the water, and especially if you're going to be distracted, for example for surf fishing or agate hunting, consider wearing a life vest and having someone spot for you so they can let you know when larger waves begin to arrive. These precautions may seem a bit extreme, but consider them like wearing your car's seat belt - you hope you never need it but it's there if you do.

So don't be afraid of visiting and enjoying our amazing north coast beaches and wild rivers. But remember to always use these simple safety tips to stay safe and stay alive.

Enjoy the long weekend!
