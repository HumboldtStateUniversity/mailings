Dear Campus Community,

In the wake of a large landslide that has closed U.S. Highway 101 approximately 5 miles north of Garberville, Calif., we urge all motorists to exercise caution when traveling through Southern Humboldt County.

The California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) advises U.S. Highway 101 could reopen to controlled, one-way traffic as early as Friday morning, but that's only if the weather stays dry.

In the mean time, the alternate route for motorists is Interstate 5, which can be reached north of the slide from Routes 36 or 299 and south of the slide by Route 20. For the most current road information on all State highways, call 1-800-427-7623 (1-800-GAS-ROAD) or visit www.dot.ca.gov. The next update is slated for Friday, April 1, at 10 a.m.

State Route 299's terminus is located just north of the Humboldt State University Campus. As of Wednesday, March 30, State Route 299 was open with no reported restrictions.

Safety is our top concern for all members of campus, and we know many of you will be traveling for the César Chávez holiday. Please check with Caltrans for highway updates and have a safe holiday.

Sincerely,

Randi Darnall Burke
Dean of Students
