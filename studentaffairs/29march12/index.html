Dear HSU Community:

This year's Humboldt State University Take Back the Night program is soon to begin; events are scheduled April 2-13. Sponsored by the HSU Womyn's Resource Center, North Coast Rape Crisis Team and the HSU Critical Race, Gender, Sexuality Studies Department, Take Back the Night is dedicated to providing supportive and healing spaces for survivors of sexualized and intimate partner violence and for their loved ones, raising consciousness and ending sexualized violence in our communities and beyond.

Please join us in a series of empowering events including the keynote address on April 10 by the beautifully inspiring social justice activist Leah Lakshmi Piepzna-Samarasinha, a queer disabled Sri Lankan writer, teacher and cultural worker who is the author of Consensual Genocide and Love Cake and co-editor of The Revolution Starts At Home: Confronting Intimate Violence in Activist Communities. Leah's keynote address will be composed of a mix of spoken word and discussions of transformative justice within the context of sexualized and intimate partner violence.

The series of events will culminate on Friday, April 13 in the Quad beginning at 5pm with the Resistance themed Rally, Gender Inclusive Speak Out, Peace Making Circles, Womyn Identified March and Alternative Space for non-marchers.

HSU is proud of our student community and of our students' commitment to social justice. We thank our students, faculty, staff and other allies for their work and encourage you to become further engaged in learning how, as individuals and as members of larger communities, we can work toward ending sexualized violence.

We look forward to your participation in building awareness about and preventing sexualized violence.

The full schedule and description of events is available on the campus events calendar and on the Womyn's Resource Center website.

Sincerely,
Peg Blake
Vice President, Enrollment Management and Student Affairs
