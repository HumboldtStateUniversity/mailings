Campus Community,

Commencement ceremonies are this Saturday, May 16, and a large number of parents, family members and friends will be traveling to campus. We're looking forward to a day of joy and celebration, which our graduates have certainly earned.

This year, a few extra precautions are necessary because of the H1N1 "swine flu."

We do not believe there is an inherent risk in participating in Commencement ceremonies. However, we urge you to be informed, share information with those who plan to visit and make sensible choices.

In particular, all individuals who plan to attend should monitor their health prior to the ceremonies. Please do not attend if you have had flu like illness in the last seven days or a known exposure to someone diagnosed as having flu.

On the day of Commencement, campus staff will complete additional cleaning of facilities between ceremonies, and graduates will have access to hand sanitizer. The flyers about hand-washing that you may have seen around campus will be posted at various event locations. And as always, medical staff will be on hand.

There have been no confirmed cases of H1N1 in Humboldt County, but health officials suspect it likely is or shortly will be established locally. The campus has taken steps to prepare for a possible local flu outbreak and to provide information on the best ways to stay healthy.

For the latest campus updates, please visit our website at www.humboldt.edu.

Regular updates are available from the Centers for Disease Control (CDC) at www.cdc.gov/swineflu and the World Health Organization at http://www.who.int/csr/disease/swineflu/en/index.html. New CDC guidelines for public gatherings are available at http://www.cdc.gov/h1n1flu/guidance/public_gatherings.htm.

Thank you for your attention and cooperation. The health and safety of our campus community is a top priority, and we all have a part to play when it comes to preventing the spread of flu.

Sincerely,

Steven Butler
