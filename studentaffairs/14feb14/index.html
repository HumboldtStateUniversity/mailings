Information You Should Know about Title IX

The Chancellor of the California State University system recently issued a series of Executive Orders (1072-4) to ensure compliance with Title IX of the Education Amendments of 1972, US Department of Education. These Executive Orders classify almost every faculty and staff member at HSU as a “responsible employee” who is mandated to report incidents of sexual harassment and sexual violence to a campus Title IX Officer.

It is important that you know that there are still people on and off campus you can speak to confidentially about sexual harassment and/or sexual violence:

Community agencies with options for anonymous and confidential
24 hr support

North Coast Rape Crisis Team (707) 445-2881, 24 hour hotline; no mandated reporting requirements.

Humboldt Domestic Violence Services (707) 443-6042, 24 hour hotline; mandated only to report child abuse to state authorities.

On campus options for confidential support

Counseling and Psychological Services Office (707) 826-3236, including 24 hour crisis counseling; for an overview of the limits to confidentiality go to http://www.humboldt.edu/counseling/.

Mira Friedman, HSU Health Educator, (707) 826-5234; must report to UPD in cases of imminent threat to the community.

Mary Sue Savage, Prevention Coordinator (707) 826-5123; must report to UPD in cases of imminent threat to the community.

The University Police Department (707) 826-5555 offers students options for anonymous and confidential reports.

What these policies mean for students:

  • If you decide to tell an HSU staff or faculty member about an incident of sexual harassment or sexual violence and you provide information that this harm occurred while you were enrolled at HSU, the staff or faculty member (outside of those listed above who are exempt) will call Randi Darnall Burke, the Dean of Students. She or her designee will then contact you to offer you options for support, accommodations and accountability.
  • No disciplinary action will be taken against you for violating campus drug and alcohol policy if you were under the influence of alcohol or other drugs when you were harmed.
  • Students may speak and write about their experiences with sexual harassment and sexual violence in papers, classes, and in discussions with faculty and staff, and no report needs to be made unless students specify that the violence happened while they were a student at HSU.

HSU's goals in implementing these CSU policies are to link survivors of sexual violence with support, and to hold accountable those who choose to harm others.

Visit the following HSU websites for additional information: Title IX http://humboldt.edu/titleix/ and Stop Rape http://www.humboldt.edu/stoprape/
