Dear Campus Colleagues:

This year's Humboldt State University Take Back the Night program events are scheduled April 8-12. Sponsored by the HSU Women's Resource Center, North Coast Rape Crisis Team and the HSU Critical Race, Gender, Sexuality Studies Department, Take Back the Night is dedicated to providing supportive and healing spaces for survivors of sexualized and intimate partner violence and for their loved ones, raising consciousness and ending sexualized violence in our communities and beyond.

Please join us in a series of empowering events including the keynote address on April 9 by Joyce Moser, DA Victim Witness Program Coordinator, Office of the District Attorney of the County of Humboldt. Joyce has dedicated her professional career to advocating for positive change in attitudes, policies, and practices in domestic violence, crimes against children, sexual assault, and in the effective delivery of services to victims/survivors of violent crime and their families. Joyce is a member of the Yurok Tribe, the Inter-Tribal Women's Advocacy Network, the Northern California Tribal Healing Coalition, California Crime Victims Assistance Association, Humboldt Sexual Assault Response Team, Humboldt Child Abuse Services Team, Humboldt Domestic Violence Coordinating Council, Humboldt Domestic Violence Response Team and the Humboldt County District Attorney & Tribal Governments Roundtable. The series of events will culminate on Friday, April 12 in the University Center Quad beginning at 5pm with the Resistance themed Rally, Gender Inclusive Speak Out, Peace Making Circles, Womyn Identified March and Alternative Space for non-marchers.

HSU is proud of our student community and of our students' commitment to social justice. We thank our students, faculty, staff and other allies for their work and encourage you to become further engaged in learning how, as individuals and as members of larger communities, we can work toward ending sexualized violence.

We look forward to your participation in building awareness about and preventing sexualized violence.

The full schedule and description of events is available on the campus events calendar and on the Women's Resource Center website.

Sincerely,

Peg L. Blake, Ph.D.
Vice President, Enrollment Management & Student Affairs
