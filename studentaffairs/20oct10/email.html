Dear Faculty and Staff,

From time to time, you will have contact with students whose behavior causes you concern and/or discomfort. In the classroom, this behavior may also interfere with the learning of other students.

What to do? If the behavior is disruptive and could be viewed as a student disciplinary matter, the Student Affairs office is available to assist you (extension 3361) as are the University Police (extension 5555).

However, if your primary concern is with the welfare of the student–depression, weight loss, relationship problems–Counseling and Psychological Services is the department to contact (extension 3236). There is always an on-call counselor available during the hours between 8:00 a.m.-5:00 p.m. There is also a document on the Counseling and Psychological Services web site that contains useful information for working with emotionally troubled students:

http://www.humboldt.edu/counseling/Download/disruptive_students.pdf

Soon, University Police, the Dean of Students, and the department of Counseling and Psychological Services will be offering training sessions on how best to deal with emotionally troubled students. Perhaps you will be able to attend one of these sessions.

Let's hope that you will not have to use this information, but if you do we are here to help.

Edward M. Webb
Vice President, Student Affairs
