As you may be aware, early Saturday morning, there was an armed robbery on our campus. The safety and security of our students and campus is a top priority at Humboldt State, and UPD and local law enforcement are investigating the incident.

This incident is an opportunity for all of us to think about how to protect ourselves on and off campus. There are simple, common sense steps you can take to be safe:

  • Be aware of your surroundings. Recognize your vulnerability.
  • Report all suspicious persons and activities to UPD by calling 5555 from any campus phone, (707)-826-5555 from off campus or by using one of the blue call boxes on campus. In case of emergencies, dial 911.
  • Late at night travel in groups. University police offers a 24 hour, no questions asked escort service for anyone on campus. If you are alone, or even in a group and would feel safer with an escort, do not hesitate to call UPD at the numbers above.
  • Keep your property locked up. Be sure to lock your car, bike or home. Many property crimes are crimes of opportunity, so be sure to reduce your risk of theft by making it harder for thieves.
  • When in doubt, use caution. If something doesn’t feel right, if a situation doesn’t seem safe, don’t risk it. Whether it’s a person you don’t recognize hanging around outside campus housing, or some other suspicious activity, first protect yourself, and then contact UPD to report it or request assistance.

All of us are committed to making Humboldt State a safe place to learn. As we head into finals week, please be aware, be smart and be safe.

Sincerely,
