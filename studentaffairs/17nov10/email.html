Dear Students,

As many of you finalize your plans to travel for the Thanksgiving holiday break, please be sure that you are prepared for an unusually cold storm and possible snow coming to the North Coast. All of us here at Humboldt State care about your safety and want to keep you informed!

Valley rain and mountain snow will begin Wednesday night. As the storm passes through the north state, temperatures will drop, and there will be snow falling on the highway mountain passes Friday through Sunday.

YOU MAY ENCOUNTER BLACK ICE, HAIL, AND SNOW ON HIGHWAYS 101, 299, 3, and 36 THIS WEEKEND.

Before you hit the road, please be sure that you are carrying traction devices such as chains or cables for your car, and reduce your speed. It is a good idea to consult the National Weather Service at 707-443-7062, and listen to NOAA weather radio or online at http://www.wrh.noaa.gov/eka/. Road conditions are available by phoning 800-427-7623 or online at http://www.dot.ca.gov/cgi-bin/roads.cgi.

You should know that the highest elevations of Highway 101 approach 2,000 feet above sea level, and that the passes on Highway 299 and 36 exceed 3,000 feet. These elevations are expected to receive snow on Friday, Saturday, and Sunday.

Drive safely and enjoy your holiday.

Edward "Buzz" Webb
Vice President for Student Affairs
