A Message from Humboldt State University

December 14, 2020

HSU Announces New Employee Assistance Program

Human Resources at Humboldt State is excited to announce a new and improved Employee Assistance Program (EAP) called LifeMatters! We have contracted with Empathia, Inc. to provide this free, confidential service to stateside HSU (non-student) employees, regardless of timebase or permanency status.

This move marks a significant increase in health and well-being related services and resources available to our employees and their families. Information regarding a comprehensive introduction to benefits workshop hosted by Empathia will be forthcoming.

As of December 1, 2020, you can call 1-800-367-7474, 24 hours a day, every day of the year to receive LifeMatters EAP services. Professional counselors are available at all times to provide assistance to you and your eligible dependents for a wide variety of issues including:

  • Job Concerns
  • Legal or Financial Worries
  • Alcohol and Drug Abuse Problems
  • Relationship Issues
  • Emotional and Stress Related Issues
  • Family Needs
  • See the employee guide for additional information

Counseling services are available at no cost to you, your dependents, and anyone permanently residing in your household. Each eligible member is eligible for eight counseling sessions, per issue, per year. If additional services are recommended, the counselor can help you find providers who are covered by your insurance plan. These services are 100% confidential and will not be shared with anyone on the HSU campus.

Additional access to LifeMatters is available through their website, mylifematters.com. Enter the password jackswellness2 to obtain educational information, useful links, help locating child and elder care providers, self-assessment tests and much more!

Please visit HSU’s EAP Webpage for more information and resources.

In addition to the excellent employee benefits, there are additional resources for managers related to the managerial aspect of their work. Some of these additional resources include:

  • Management Consultation: Personalized assistance to address employee personal and/or performance issues. Depending on your policy, interventions to manage performance may include personal, performance, mandatory and/or fitness for duty
  • Trauma Response: Individualized assistance for responding to a wide range of traumatic workplace events. Interventions range from the distribution of materials to on-site triage and the provision of individual and/or group debriefing. Consultation: Personalized assistance to address employee personal and/or performance issues. Depending on your policy, interventions to manage performance may include personal, performance, mandatory and/or fitness for duty
  • Return to Work Conferences: A discussion to address the needs of the individual and the organization after an employee’s prolonged absence from the workplace.
  • Training and Education Programs: Interactive sessions on a wide range of topics targeting issues that affect employees, management personnel and the workplace.

Access to LifeMatters resources is available through their website, mylifematters.com. Enter the password jackswellness2 and click on the “Workplace” tile for management resources.

If you have any questions about this new service, contact Human Resources at hsuhr@humboldt.edu. You can also call LifeMatters at 1-800-367-7474 24/7 to speak with a counselor.

We hope you take advantage of this excellent benefit.
