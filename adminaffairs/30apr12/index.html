Environmental Health & Safety, together with Training and Professional Development, have developed an on-line training class in compliance with the California Code of Regulations Title 8, ยง3203. This regulation requires every California employer to establish, implement and maintain an effective Injury and Illness Prevention Program (IIPP). It is the foundation for all safety related activities within the workplace environment and helps insure regulatory compliance. This training is mandatory and every employee of Humboldt State University must complete this training prior to the end of the Fall Semester 2012. Once the training is complete, the regulatory requirement has been satisfied and the first phase of this program has been completed. There is no annual refresher requirement for this overview.

To access this training, go to the HSU Training and Professional Development website at: http://training.humboldt.edu/. Click on the "Enroll Now" link to connect to HSU's Moodle site. Users must login and self-enroll in the training.

The second phase of this program is to develop departmental IIPP's. Because the University setting is complex and diverse, each department must develop, implement and maintain its own departmental IIPP, based on the campus plan. If you are assigned further responsibility to develop the departmental IIPP, information will be provided to begin the process of developing a plan. A copy of the campus IIPP and the necessary form templates can be found by visiting the EH&S website at: http://www.humboldt.edu/ehos and selecting the "Injury and Illness (IIP) Plan" link.

Questions regarding plan development, implementation and maintenance should be directed to the EH&S Coordinator, Tom Manoli, by calling extension 5711 or emailing tfm7001@humboldt.edu.
