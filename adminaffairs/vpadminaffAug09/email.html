To ease campus access during late August, 2009, Humboldt State University is urging students, staff and faculty to explore alternative transportation when traveling to campus from Wednesday, Aug. 19 to Friday, Aug. 21.

Beginning Thursday, South Rossow Street and the entire length of Harpst Street will be closed for repaving from 6 a.m. Thursday until Friday night.

Harpst Street between LK Wood Boulevard and B Street will be closed to all vehicles and access to the Harry Griffith Hall parking lots and the Plant Operations yard will not be permitted.

For more information regarding the street closure, visit Humboldt State NOW.

Download a campus map of affected areas (pdf).

Use the following tips to avoid traffic delays:

  • Allow more time to arrive on campus
  • Utilize the Jack Pass, which is $60 per semester for staff and faculty.
  • Between Aug. 19 and 28, all HSU-related passengers can ride the HTA and RTS busses free of charge. This is a great opportunity to try out the Jack Pass without paying $60. Riders must present a complementary Jack Pass, available from University Police or the Parking Booth located on North Rossow Street.
  • Carpool, stop by the Parking Booth on North Rossow with your 2-3 passengers still onboard, and you will be given a day pass to park at any HSU metered spot for free.
  • Bike to campus
  • Utilize the 14th and Union Lots on Aug. 20 and 21.
