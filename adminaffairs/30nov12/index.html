Dear HSU Faculty, Staff and Students:

Forecasts are calling for a series of storms on the North Coast through early next week, and Humboldt State University advises students, faculty and staff to be prepared.

The storms are expected to include strong winds and gusts up to 50 miles per hour, as well as abundant rainfall. There is also the potential for minor flooding along streams and rivers.

All members of the campus community are urged to exercise caution and to monitor weather reports. It is advisable to ensure you are prepared with items such as battery-powered lighting, radios, bottled water, non-perishable food, first aid supplies, medications and other essentials.

The University expects to continue normal operations throughout the storms, with classes and activities proceeding as usual.

Updates, if necessary, will be posted on this website, on 826-INFO and on KHSU 90.5 FM radio. For the latest weather forecasts and updates, visit www.wrh.noaa.gov/eka.

If you have a maintenance concern, please contact Plant Operations Work Control at ext. 4475.

Click here for information regarding procedures to follow if the power goes out. If the campus does lose power during this storm, Plant Operations is notified immediately of the outage. They work diligently with PG&E to restore power as quickly as possible.

Please do not contact the University Police Department for power outages; these are handled through Plant Operations.

If it is an emergency and/or after hours, please contact UPD at ext. 5555. Also, please remember to register your cell phone to receive text messages regarding emergency situations on-campus. For information on this program, please visit: http://www.humboldt.edu/its/node/14

Emergency Management Organization (EMO) Personnel, remember to turn on your radios to the EOC Gold Channel to receive communication during both power and telephone outages.

Joyce Lopes, Emergency Executive
Vice President for Administrative Affairs and Finance
