Dear HSU Faculty, Staff and Students:

Our Spring Semester campus-wide TEST of our emergency alert system will take place from 11:45 AM to 12:00 PM on Wednesday, March 23.

There will be NO evacuations; this is ONLY A TEST of our alert systems.

Throughout the day, you may notice "Test Today" signage on and around the campus. Select (click on) special-colored informational boxes on the www.humboldt.edu homepage, and hear public announcements on KHSU 90.5 FM and on 826-INFO (4636).

Between 11:45 AM and 12:00 PM on Wednesday, you may hear a two-tone, high-low electronic alert sound, a public address announcement that this is a test, and a long steady tone that serves as an "all-clear" signal. You should receive an emergency text "TEST" message on your cell phone, and you will see changes on the HSU homepage. If you don't receive the "TEST" message, please be sure that you have registered your current phone number and cell carrier for emergency text messaging through HSU's online Account Center. FAQ's about registration for this free service are at www.humboldt.edu/emergency/faqs#how.

In the event that there was a real emergency that affected most or all of the campus community, the alert and informational system would provide you with notice that there is an emergency condition, and with instructions as to what you should do. Remember that the best source for the most current, detailed information about any emergency affecting HSU is www.humboldt.edu/emergency.

Remember you can register your cell phone by going to: www.humboldt.edu/emergency.

Feedback concerning this test/drill may be submitted to response@humboldt.edu.

Burt N. Nordstrom, Emergency Executive
Vice President for Administrative Affairs
