Dear HSU Students, Faculty and Staff

The Fall Semester campus-wide TEST of our emergency alert system will take place from 10:45 a.m. to 11 a.m. on Tuesday, Dec. 7.

There will be NO evacuations; this is ONLY A TEST of our alert systems.

Throughout the day, you may notice "Test Today" signage on and around campus, special-colored informational boxes on the www.humboldt.edu homepage, and hear public announcements on KHSU 90.5 FM and on 826-INFO (4636).

Between 10:45 and 11 a.m. on Tuesday, you may hear a two-tone, high-low electronic alert sound, a public address announcement that this is a test, and a long steady tone that serves as an "all-clear" signal. You should receive an emergency text "TEST" message on your cell phone, if you are registered, and you will see changes on the HSU homepage during that period.

If you do not receive the "TEST" message, please be sure that you have registered your current phone number and cell carrier for emergency text messaging through HSU's Account Center at http://www.humboldt.edu/emergency . Frequently asked questions about registration for this free service can be found at http://www.humboldt.edu/emergency/faqs#how.

In addition to the campus-wide test on Tuesday, there may be additional sounds and test announcements briefly heard throughout the day as technicians test and calibrate speakers.

If there was a real emergency that affected most or all of the campus community, the alert and informational system would provide you with notice that there is an emergency condition, and with instructions as to what you should do. Remember that the best source for the most current, detailed information about any emergency affecting HSU is http://www.humboldt.edu/emergency.

Feedback concerning this test/drill may be submitted to response@humboldt.edu.

Thank you,

Burt N. Nordstrom, Emergency Executive
Vice President for Administrative Affairs
