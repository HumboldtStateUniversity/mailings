A Message from Cal Poly Humboldt

February 28, 2022

New Cal Poly Humboldt Academic Program Buildout Well Underway

Dear Campus Community,
Cal Poly Humboldt’s vision to become an innovative polytechnic rests on our excellent academic programs. To that end, the University plans to launch 12 new academic programs and has proposed an additional 15 over the next seven years.
New Academic Programs Planned to Launch by 2023
Applied Fire Science & Management B.S.; Cannabis Studies B.A.; Cybersecurity Stackable Certificate; Data Science B.S.; Energy Systems Engineering B.S.; Engineering & Community Practice M.Eng.; Equitable & Sustainable Future Certificate; Geospatial Science & Technology B.S.; Information Technology Certificate; Marine Biology B.S.; Mechanical Engineering B.S.; Software Engineering B.S.
These high-demand programs emphasize the importance of building out our STEM offerings and our liberal arts programs. Both are crucial to the success of our students and Humboldt’s future. Our academic expansion also highlights the amazing expertise of our faculty. Those faculty include members of the Curriculum & Academic Programs Working Group. The team is responsible for planning and executing new curricula and content for new academic programs, and ensuring resources are thoughtfully allocated.
Their work isn’t possible without our key partners—the Integrated Curriculum Committee and the University Senate. Curriculum and academic plans for the Fall 2023 programs are being reviewed by the ICC before final consideration and approval by the University Senate. We are excited to share that the University Senate has approved the programs below to-date:
  • Cannabis Studies, B.A.
  • Data Science, B.S.
  • Marine Biology, B.S.
The California State University Board of Trustees is expected to give final approval in March for all of our proposed Fall 2023 academic programs—the last step in the formal process. The goal is to have all programs approved by the end of the semester to ensure applicants can begin declaring majors in these programs for Fall 2023.
Developing and approving a curriculum for a single program can take up to two years. Creating new programming on this scale and on an accelerated timeline is unprecedented. We’re extremely proud of our faculty and deans who are working hard to continue providing students an amazing educational experience at Cal Poly Humboldt.
For details about the programs, please check out the website and if you have any questions, feedback, or ideas, please leave your comments in the Polytech Implementation Feedback Form.
