Message from President of Associated Students

March 15, 2019

Sent on behalf of the President of Associated Students.

Dear Humboldt State Students, Faculty, Staff, and Administrators,

Now more than ever we need your presence. If you are outraged, angered, saddened, numb, or disheartened by the recent grand jury decision, take these emotions and propel them into action with us. Collectively we have power, and together we demand justice.

Join us at the UC Quad on Friday, March 15th @ 12 PM. Please come and show up and show support for Charmaine Lawson, the mother of David Josiah Lawson.

Later in the day please also join us on the steps of the Eureka Courthouse on Friday, March 15th @ 5:30 pm. There will be carpooling at the HSU Library circle at 4:45 pm and then head to the Humboldt Courthouse from there.

The results of the survey should be particularly valuable to the new HSU President, who is expected to be appointed in time to begin this summer.

If you are willing to drive people, please meet at the Library Circle today at 4:45pm.

If you have never shown up, but always wanted to, now is the time. If you or your family have suffered an injustice or have been impacted by the Humboldt County’s DA’s office or law enforcement, we stand with you. Join us and protest. As Charmaine Lawson has said, “This is bigger than justice for my son.”

If you are tired, saddened, numb, and most impacted by this decision, we have your back, we support you. We will not stop.

Charmaine Lawson is a testament to love, strength, and faith. Be motivated and empowered by her light. She will not only fight for justice for her son, but she is also fighting for justice in Humboldt County.

When Black Lives Matter there will be true justice.
