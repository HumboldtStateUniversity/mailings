A Message from Humboldt State University

December 10, 2019

Supporting the HSU Community Following Racist Graffiti

El Centro Académico Cultural is an open space of encounter where students explore and express shared values, differing experiences, distinct histories, and multidimensional identities to foster powerful individuals and communities. As a space of encounter, our center is a place of support for people who want to connect with each other and affirm the dignity of difference needed to manifest a community where students can develop their academic, intellectual, personal and professional growth.

Last Friday the University Police Department received a report of racist graffiti targeting our immigrant community in the Art Building. This kind of hateful aggression can not and will not be tolerated on campus.

We recognize that these types of abhorrent and vile acts work to hinder and obstruct the full potential of our community. We therefore call on our campus community to stand in solidarity to affirm that we are a community that will not tolerate hate and racism. We invite you to come to El Centro (NHE 205) this Friday, December 13th at 12pm to discuss this incident and work together to build a better future. We also want to extend our support if anyone would like to discuss this incident throughout the week prior to the Friday gathering you are welcome to come by the center to meet with our front desk student rep, and/or with the center coordinators.

When our community is attacked we need to stand together, supporting one another and we at El Centro are here for you.
