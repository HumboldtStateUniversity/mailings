A Message from Humboldt State University

September 17, 2018

AS President Welcome Letter

The following is being sent on behalf of Associated Students.

Dear Campus Community,

Welcome to all the new folx and returning folx to HSU. I hope classes are going well and you all are settling in.

I would first like to acknowledge that our university is situated on stolen Wiyot land. We should keep the Wiyot tribe in our thoughts, and include their struggles in our history (present and past) as a campus.

My name is Jazmin Sandoval and I am your 2018-2019 Associated Students (AS) President. I transferred to HSU last year from Los Angeles Valley College and I am majoring in film. My journey here has been a whirlwind of events. I have met so many wonderful people who have helped me grow and learn. The Womxn’s Resource Center and the Multicultural Center have been a safe haven for me to express myself and meet new people as well as Mecha and CCAT. This is my senior year at HSU and I look forward to serving and representing ya’ll as the new AS President.

Additionally, the North Coast Rape Crisis Team offers free and confidential survivor focused advocacy for survivors of sexual assault. You may reach NCRCT at via their 24-hour hotlines at Humboldt: 707-445-288 and Del Norte: 707-465-2851

So, what is AS you ask? Well, it is the official student voice on campus; so we are your student government. You fund us and we advocate for you all! We are located on the southside of the second floor of the University Center building; directly below the Bookstore and above The Depot. Please feel free to drop by anytime Monday-Friday 8am-5pm and say hello! We are here for YOU.

As AS President I have three main priorities this year :

  1. Building more communication and listening between administration , faculty, staff, the community that surrounds us, and of course you, the students. I am going to be as transparent as possible and will push for accountability.
  2. Helping prioritize HSU’s most vulnerable and underrepresented students which includes demanding justice for HSU student David Josiah Lawson’s murder last year on April 15, 2017 (if you are not aware please take the time to read up on his case ), and following/pressuring City Council and law enforcement to bring Justice for Josiah and his family.
  3. Providing more resources, access and information regarding basic needs (housing insecurity, food insecurity, mental health services, financial aid) like the Student Housing Advocate Alliance, Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) , and OhSnap!.

We have an AS position that you can still apply for if interested:
- Social Justice and Equity Officer (Executive; Board of Directors voting member): Chief advisor to the A.S. President and the A.S. Council on matters of Social Justice and Equity.

There are also over 100 committees that students can be on. These committees are HUGE as they make a lot of decisions that affect us all so I highly recommend applying at the HSU AS website under the Get Involved tab . Any student can apply for committees and someone from AS will interview you, appoint you, and then you can be on the committees. Many of these committees have little to no student representation so I really want to express to you all how these committees are so important. Many of these committees are also open to the public and students can sit in and listen. Examples of some of these committees are the University Senate, University Center, URPC and Instructionally Related Activities committee which either allocates funds, makes decisions, or creates policy and legislation for HSU. As your new student body president I also want to educate and inform you all on where your student fee money goes to if you did not already know.

Here is the break down:
In addition to tuition fee of $5,742 for full-time resident undergrads, last year each student paid a total of $1,746 toward student fees . From the $1,746, each one of you paid individually, $117 went towards AS to fund programs and services on campus that are for YOU (CCAT, ERC, MCC, WRC, YES, SWB, WRRAP, etc). In total AS had a student fee revenue of $957,000 last year. We allocated that towards different programs and services on campus. To learn more about tution and fees check out this link: https://www2.humboldt.edu/financialservices/node/45

Be on the lookout for a Student Budget Forum where AS will provide more in depth details regarding the 2018-19 budget as well as a Graduation Initiative 2025 Forum which is an initiative started by the CSU system to close the opportunity and achievement gap by the year 2025 through a $75 million budget that can be allocated for either these six categories: Academic Preparation, Enrollment Management, Student Engagement and Well-being, Financial Aid, Administrative Barriers, and Data-Driven Decision Making. HSU was given $2.4 million of the $74 million and have $1.4 million left to allocate. The Student Success Alliance Committee is in charge of allocating that money and I sit on that committee. I would like more student input so please reach out to me if you have any questions or suggestions.

I want to educate us all so we are all informed on what is going on campus by making videos, reaching out, and being as involved as possible to learn from you all on how to make it better, and make HSU as welcoming and inclusive as possible. I think the budget is important and I would love to hear from you all on what else you would like to know about. If there is anything you may need please come to us, or we can come to you (however you prefer) ! We are here as a resource to you and know it is your right as a student to feel safe, included, and protected here at Humboldt State University. I have attached wonderful AS/HSU/Community events coming up that anyone is welcome to attend and many are FREE.

Lastly, I had the pleasure and privilege of attending California State Student Association (CSSA) meetings over the summer. CSSA is the official voice for students of all 23 CSU campuses. They advocate for access to affordable and high quality CSU education. CSSA works to ensure students are at the forefront of higher education policy in California so WE ARE ALL CSSA.

Additionally, Isabel Sanchez-Pacheco, a senior at HSU, was appointed this month by the CSSA Board of Directors to be the CSSA Sustainability Officer . In this position, Isabel represents the voice of all 480,000 students at the state level around the topic of environmental sustainability in the CSU system. Isabel is excited to use their experience as a compost site operator at the Waste Reduction and Resource Program (WRRAP) and as a co-director at the Campus Center for Appropriate Technology (CCAT) to help other universities implement these ideas into their campuses. If you have any questions, comments, concerns about sustainability on this campus or in the CSU system, contact them at Ijs58@humboldt.edu.

With that being said CSSA finalized the policy agenda for the new 2018-19 academic year and I wanted you all to be aware of it so here is the CSSA 2018-19 Policy Agenda:

  1. Enable access to housing, food, and wellness resources that promote overall student health and safety.
  2. Ensure that the CSU is accessible, affordable, and sustainable.
  3. Ensure the academic success and holistic educational experience of all CSU students.

I hope you all find your time here valuable and enjoyable. I want you to know you matter, not just as a student, but as a human being on this planet. We can make our communities better and I hope to find growth and peace in this wild journey at HSU. Please never feel ashamed, embarrassed, or a bother to ask as many questions as possible and know you belong here and we all deserve to feel that way. The University would not exist without you, STUDENTS FIRST. My tips of advice in getting through the year is practice self care (your mental health matters more than that assignment, no matter what!) and even if this University overwhelms you, know that I am here for you and so are many others. Also don’t forget to tune into KRFH 105.1 FM, HSU’s very own student run radio!

Thank you kindly for reading and being you,

Jazmin Sandoval
