A Message from Humboldt State University

January 21, 2022

Student Forum Recording: Associated Students Strategic Plan and Polytechnic Presentation

Dear Campus Community,
Humboldt State University students joined a productive virtual forum to learn more about the student governing body, Associated Students (A.S.), and the University’s polytechnic transition process.
The event featured panelists A.S. President Jeremiah Finley and leads from all seven polytechnic implementation teams: (Budget, Finance & Reporting Working Group; Communications Working Group; Curriculum and Academic Programs Working Group; Enrollment and Growth Management Working Group; Facilities Working Group; Inclusive Student Success/GI 2025 Working Group; Technology and Infrastructure Working Group). (Read more about the teams and their charges).
Students heard a general overview of A.S., including its purpose, the resources it provides, and its student advocacy work, in addition to the organization’s 2021-2026 Strategic Plan–a roadmap of goals and how to achieve them. To address frequently asked questions about the polytechnic designation, a separate presentation detailed the reasons and benefits of such a designation, budget and funding priorities, academic programs, and infrastructure projects. Students also had the opportunity to ask panelists questions about everything from becoming a Student Trustee for the California State University to polytech state funding.
You can expect more details about these topics, plus additional open forums and opportunities to provide feedback in future communications. Students, faculty, and staff are welcome to read the A.S. Strategic Plan, watch a recording of Wednesday’s forum, and review the polytechnic slideshow. Also feel free to refer to the polytechnic website for updates, background information, and more.
On Wednesday, Jan. 26, please don’t forget to watch the CSU Board of Trustees meeting for the presentation on the final proposal for the University’s name to be changed to California State Polytechnic University, Humboldt.
Sincerely,
Jeremiah Finley
President for Associated Students
Jenn Capps
Provost and Vice President for Academic Affairs
