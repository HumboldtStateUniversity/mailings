A Message from Humboldt State University

December 7, 2017

Loss Of HSU Professor Patty Siering

Dear University Community:

We are saddened to share news of the loss of Humboldt State Biological Sciences Professor Patty Siering, who died of cancer Dec. 3. She was 55. We extend our condolences to her husband, Biological Sciences Professor Mark Wilson, and to her loved ones.

Patty made major contributions to understanding bacteria in nature. Her interest in microbiology began in the 1980s, a time when few scientists studied natural microbial communities. She pursued her passion in a doctoral program at Cornell University, where she not only developed into an outstanding microbiologist but also met Mark.

Patty was offered a faculty position at Humboldt State and Johns Hopkins University. She chose HSU because of its emphasis on teaching and her love of the area. During her 19-year tenure here, she founded and led a diverse team of scientists that studied microorganisms living in hydrothermal pools at Lassen Volcanic National Park. The team’s research was integrated into courses at HSU and other collaborating institutions.

Colleagues remember Patty as a cheerful person with a kind and strong spirit. She was also a dedicated teacher and mentor who provided research opportunities to hundreds of students. Her legacy will continue through the Siering/Wilson Research Endowment, which she and Mark established recently.

Patty’s death is a loss for the Humboldt State community and we are thinking of her and those who are close to her.
