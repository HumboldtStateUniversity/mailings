A Message from Humboldt State University

April 9, 2019

Loss of HSU Student Sadie Breana Brennan

Dear University Community,

We are sad to share news of the loss of Humboldt State University student Sadie Breana Brennan. We extend our condolences to all who have been affected by her loss, particularly her family members and friends.

Sadie, a Political Science major from Taylorsville, California, died on Sunday, April 7. She was 28 years old.

Sadie’s death is a difficult loss for our community, and University staff are working to provide support to those on campus who knew her. They have also been in close contact with Sadie’s family and will continue to assist them during this difficult time.

For those who are struggling with grief or otherwise, know that help is available. Students can contact Counseling & Psychological Services at 707.826.3236 anytime. The Dean of Students Office may be reached at 707.826.3504. Staff or faculty seeking additional support may utilize the Employee Assistance Program at 707.443.7358.

As always, if you are in crisis (e.g., having thoughts of suicide), we encourage you to contact 800.273.TALK (8255) or text HOME to 741741--both are free and confidential resources that are available 24/7. In case of immediate safety issues or as otherwise appropriate, the University Police Department may be reached 24 hours a day at 707.826.5555 or dial 911 in an emergency.
