A Message from Cal Poly Humboldt

September 30, 2022

Suspicious Packages Sent to Faculty

Dear Campus Community:
On Thursday afternoon several faculty members from the Critical Race, Gender & Sexuality Studies program received packages, each containing the same book with hateful, misogynistic, homophobic, and other negative content. The packages were collected by the Dean of the College of Arts Humanities & Social Sciences and handed over to the University Police Department (UPD).
UPD analyzed the packages and determined that there is no imminent or immediate threat to the campus community. UPD is currently investigating the incident and will share the packages with federal agencies for further review.
If you see or receive packages with the Supreme Court or Chief Justice John Roberts as the return address, please leave the package where it is and immediately contact UPD officer Andy Martin at jam1855@humboldt.edu or (707) 826-5555. Faculty may also contact their respective dean, chair, or supervisor to ensure they are aware of the situation.
We do not tolerate acts of hatred and bigotry. We are deeply committed to standing beside and with our colleagues who are doing the difficult work of teaching and leading in these crucial disciplines.
Incidents like these can be deeply distressing. Staff or faculty seeking additional support may utilize the Employee Assistance Program, LifeMatters, offered through Empathia 24 hours a day, seven days a week. Counseling & Psychological Services (CAPS) has more information on counseling for students.
If you have concerns or questions feel free to contact us directly. We will keep you apprised as we learn more.
Sincerely,

Jenn Capps
Provost and Vice President for Academic Affairs
