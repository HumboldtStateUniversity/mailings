Faculty & Staff Participation
Processional Participation
Faculty and staff who plan to participate in the processional and be seated in the designated faculty and staff section during a Commencement ceremony must register their processional participation by April 15.
The College Office Coordinators will be coordinating the student and faculty/staff line-up in the West Gym and will be providing information and updates to faculty & staff registrants.
Commencement Support
Employees are needed to assist at Commencement behind the scenes. This includes administrators, faculty, staff, student assistants (and even volunteers). Roles include greeting guests, ushering, providing shuttle and wheelchair escort support, assisting with parking and traffic, and helping in the graduate and faculty/staff lineup areas. Those who would like to work Commencement are asked to make arrangements with their supervisor for their work hours and submit their Commencement work interest and availability by April 15. Details about shift needs and assignments will be shared closer to the event.
More information on faculty and staff participation can be found at commencement.humboldt.edu/faculty-staff.

Clear Bag Guidelines
Starting in December 2025, large campus events—including Commencement—follow new clear bag guidelines and additional security measures to help speed up entry and keep the event safe for graduates, families, and guests at all venues.
Key Details
Bags larger than 8.5” x 6” must be clear (transparent plastic, vinyl, or PVC). Opaque wallets or clutches smaller than 8.5” x 6” are permitted and are subject to search.
Opaque, oversized bags, such as backpacks, tote bags, large purses, camera bags, and similar items, will not be permitted inside the venue. Accommodations will be made for medically necessary items, diaper bags, and strollers. These items will be subject to inspection. It is recommended not to bring a bag, if possible, to expedite entry.
These steps are becoming standard at universities and venues across the country. They’re simple, but they go a long way in helping us create a safe, efficient, and positive environment for our graduates and their families. See the FAQs on the Commencement website for more information.
