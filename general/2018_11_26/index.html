A Message from Humboldt State University

November 26, 2018

Loss of HSU Student Kaylie Kloss

Dear University Community:

We are sad to share news of the loss of Humboldt State University student Kaylie Kloss. We extend our condolences to all who have been affected by her loss, particularly her family members and friends. Kaylie’s death is a difficult loss for our community, and our thoughts are with all of those who are close to her.

Kaylie, an Oceanography major from Garden Grove, California, died over the weekend. She was 21 years old.

University staff are working to provide support to those on campus who knew her. They are also reaching out to Kaylie’s family, and will continue to assist them in any way possible during this difficult time.

For those who are struggling with grief or otherwise, know that help is available. Students can contact Counseling & Psychological Services at 707.826.3236 anytime. The Dean of Students Office may be reached at 707.826.3504. Staff or faculty seeking additional support may utilize the Employee Assistance Program at 707.443.7358.

As always, anyone struggling with thoughts of suicide are encouraged to use the additional resources of 800.273.TALK (8255) or text HOME to 741741, both are free and confidential resources that are available 24/7. In case of immediate safety issues or as otherwise appropriate, the University Police Department may be reached 24 hours a day at 707.826.5555 or dial 911 in an emergency.
