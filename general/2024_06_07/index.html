A Message from Cal Poly Humboldt

June 7, 2024

Loss of Jeshua Wilkinson

Dear Campus Community,
We are saddened to share news of the loss of Jeshua Wilkinson.
Jeshua’s death is a loss for the Cal Poly Humboldt community, and our thoughts are with all of those who are close to him.
Jeshua was a senior Kinesiology major and worked in the Human Performance Lab as part of a team of four. He led and assisted in health and fitness testing for members of the University and the surrounding community.
Jeshua is remembered as a hard worker and a truly kind human. He demonstrated excellent leadership and professional skills and worked hard to improve his understanding and abilities in order to progress to the next stage of his education. Jeshua’s professors say he was a pleasure to have in class. He will be missed.
Support services are available for students through Counseling & Psychological Services (CAPS) and via telehealth at TimelyCare (for both immediate and scheduled care). If you have not yet registered for telehealth through TimelyCare, you may register at timelycare.com/calpolyhumboldt with your name and your University email address. This will enable you to schedule visits (including a “TalkNow” session) from any web-enabled device, smartphone, laptop, or desktop.
Psychological support services are available for faculty and staff through the Employee Assistance Program (EAP), which provides free confidential counseling sessions through Empathia 24 hours a day, seven days a week.
