A Message from Humboldt State University

March 4, 2020

Campus COVID-19 Planning and Preparations

As HSU last communicated on February 26, the University is taking several measures to help protect the health, safety, and wellbeing of our campus community as the coronavirus (COVID-19) spreads globally. HSU has:

  • Launched a website (covid-19.humboldt.edu), where you can find updates, and links to health recommendations, travel advisories and other related information.
  • Activated HSU’s Emergency Operations Center, a group of trained staff who plan, prepare, and respond to unexpected events that may disrupt daily campus operations.
  • Organized the Pandemic Planning Committee. To help plan for the continuity of classes and daily operations, this group of campus stakeholders is discussing academic, personnel, and operational policies and procedures.
  • Launched an educational campaign to raise awareness of proper handwashing and other ways to reduce the risk of infecting yourself and others.
  • Working with state, county, and CSU officials to coordinate a response to the potential outbreak.

Information on the coronavirus changes quickly and can feel alarming. While public awareness is crucial, health experts recommend taking basic steps to protect yourself and to help prevent the spread of the coronavirus or other illnesses.

  • Hygiene and Sanitation: The CDC recommends washing hands frequently and using hand sanitizer. Avoid touching your face, cover your mouth when you sneeze or cough. Clean frequently touched surfaces and objects.
  • Stay home if you’re sick.
  • Surgical and N95 Masks: Wearing a mask isn’t necessary if you’re healthy, according to the Centers for Disease Control. An N95 mask (also referred to as a medical respirator) is recommended only for use by healthcare personnel who need protection from airborne and fluid hazards (e.g., splashes, sprays). More from the CDC on differences between a surgical mask and N95 mask.

As we prepare for this virus and its impact, it’s important to have compassion for ourselves and others.
