Dear Campus Community,

I strongly urge all current HSU students, staff, and faculty aged 24 and younger to participate in one of the following H1N1 (swine) flu NO-COST vaccination events offered at the following campus venues:

  • Friday, Nov 13 Jolly Giant Commons 2nd Floor 10:00 AM-4:00 PM
  • Tuesday, Nov 17 University Center South Lounge 9:00 AM-6:00 PM
  • Wednesday, Nov 18 Jolly Giant Commons 2nd Floor 10:00 AM-7:00 PM

These vaccination clinics are the outcome of collaboration among federal, state, county, and HSU authorities. Strict guidelines are in place regarding who is eligible (and who is not) for this series of HSU community vaccinations.

Eligible Group: The ONLY persons who will be vaccinated at these venues must meet ALL of the following criteria:

  • Current HSU student, staff, or faculty (bring current HSU ID card)
  • Age 24 or younger on the day of the vaccination (bring government-issued photo ID or driver's license with date of birth; students aged 17 or younger must also present a consent form signed by a parent or guardian, available at http://studentaffairs.humboldt.edu/_download/registration_consent.pdf.)
  • Must bring both ID's to get vaccinated

Persons NOT eligible for H1N1 vaccination:

  • Spouses, significant others, children, friends, and colleagues who are not current HSU students, staff, or faculty aged 24 or younger
  • Alumni, retired, and emeritus HSU employees
  • People who have a severe allergy to chicken eggs
  • People who have had a severe reaction to an influenza vaccination
  • People who developed Guillain-Barré syndrome (GBS) within 6 weeks of getting an influenza vaccine previously

Those who do not meet the eligibility criteria above should contact their own health care practitioner about H1N1 vaccination. The Centers for Disease Control anticipates that eventually there will be enough vaccine distributed for everyone who wishes to be vaccinated, but not everyone will be able to receive it on campus. As supplies become available, vaccine will be distributed through private practitioners in the coming months.

If you have recently had the flu, you are still eligible to get vaccinated unless you had a definitive lab test confirming that you had H1N1 flu.

The upcoming HSU vaccinations will be dependent on the supply and demand. Over 2,000 doses of vaccine have been secured for the upcoming campus vaccinations. Only one dose of H1N1 vaccine per person should be needed to adequately protect the targeted group being vaccinated on campus. The vaccinations at these HSU venues will be administered by a nurse.

The H1N1 vaccine has been evaluated by many agencies and experts, and has been deemed extremely safe. More information about safety of flu vaccine, links to important websites, and answers to frequently asked questions about H1N1 vaccination may be found at www.humboldt.edu/flu.

About ten days after vaccination, the body begins to produce antibodies providing protection from the H1N1 virus. So, the sooner the better to start getting vaccine to our HSU community members. If you are eligible for vaccination, please take advantage of one of the upcoming free vaccination events.

Sincerely,

Rebecca Stauffer MD
