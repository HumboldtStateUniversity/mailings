A Message from Humboldt State University

November 3, 2020

Election Day and HSU Values

Dear Campus Community:

Today is Election Day when millions of Americans will exercise one of our most fundamental rights and responsibilities - voting for our representatives as well as on local and state ballot measures. If you have not already done so, please make sure your voice is heard by casting your vote. This is an important part of working for change locally and nationally.

At HSU, civic engagement characterizes much of the work of our students, faculty, and staff through courses, clubs, organizations, and initiatives on campus and in their own communities. This dedication is a reflection of the character of our campus community. It’s also an extension of the University’s commitment to a learning experience that fosters in students an ability to examine with a critical eye social justice, equity, and environmental issues so they may become agents of the changes to create a society that is just and sustainable.

We are very proud of the hundreds of campus community members who worked to raise awareness about the election process and to increase engagement and voting among students. If recent elections are any indication of turnout, HSU students will be voting in large, possibly, historic numbers.

The pandemic presents some unique voter challenges. If you are voting in person, please remember to wear a mask and keep a safe distance from others. The CDC’s website has health and safety tips for voters.

This has been a tumultuous and challenging year for many reasons. Regardless of the divisive politics in our nation, HSU remains committed to our core values.

  • We will be renowned for social and environmental responsibility and action.
  • We believe the key to our common future will be the individual citizen who acts in good conscience and engages in informed action.
  • We will commit to increasing our diversity of people and perspectives.
  • We will be exemplary partners with our communities, including tribal nations.
  • We believe in the dignity of all individuals, in fair and equitable treatment, and in equal opportunity. We value the richness and interplay of differences. We value the inclusiveness of diversity, and we respect alternative paradigms of thought.
  • We believe individuals must be environmentally, economically and socially responsible in the quest for viable and sustainable communities.

Not everyone will agree with the outcome of national and state elections. However, it’s paramount to continue engaging in respectful and collegial dialogue as a university community. If you decide to exercise your rights to speak up about the election results or join with others in celebration or protest, we ask that you do your part to contribute to a safe and respectful environment and experience for everyone involved.

For students, the Office of Student Life is hosting virtual Student Election Dialogues on Wednesday, Nov. 4 at 5 p.m. (register) and Wednesday, Nov. 12 at noon (register). For details, see the HSU Voter Information page.
