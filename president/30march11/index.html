Dear Campus Community,

Join me in congratulating Professor Leslie VanderMolen, the 2010/2011 Excellence in Teaching Award – Lecturer recipient, and Professor Saeed Mortazavi, the 2010/2011 Outstanding Service Award recipient, as recommended by the Academic Senate.

Professor VanderMolen has been a biological sciences lecturer at HSU for 20 years and is known for her high level of commitment, passion, and dedication to effectively engaging students. Her nomination was based on the "consistent excellence of her teaching" and the "well documented impact that she has had on her students" as noted by the Faculty Awards Committee members.

Professor Saeed Mortazavi from the School of Business is being recognized for his "sustained and distinguished service to HSU, the community, and the profession" for the past 28 years, serving as a member of 35 different university-level committees.

These individuals have been identified by their colleagues as among the very strongest faculty we have at HSU. Please join me to celebrate their accomplishments and what they have meant to our university at a reception on Tuesday, May 3, 5:00-6:30 at Baywood Golf and Country Club. Please contact Kelly Mathson to RSVP for the reception, 826-3311, kellym@humboldt.edu no later than Thursday, April 28. Professors VanderMolen and Mortazavi will also be invited to present a public lecture during the 2011-2012 academic year.

Please also join me in congratulating recipients of the President Alistair McCrone Promising Faculty Scholars Award who are being recognized this semester by the Sponsored Programs Foundation. Congratulations to Professor Matthew Hurst, Department of Chemistry, Professor Alexandru Tomescu, Department of Biological Sciences, and Professor Rosemary Sherriff, Department of Geography, for their scholarly achievements and exceptional contributions to their field.
