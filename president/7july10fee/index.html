Dear Students,

The California State University Board of Trustees recently voted to raise the State University Fee by 5 percent for undergraduates and graduate professional programs. For full-time undergraduates, that translates into a $204 annual increase.

Beginning Fall 2010, the total annual mandatory fees for full-time undergraduates at HSU will be $5,751, which accounts for both the system fee increase and recent changes to HSU campus-based fees. Total fees for full-time resident credential program participants will be $6,429 and for full-time resident graduate students, $6,735.

After the vote to increase fees, CSU Chancellor Charles B. Reed explained that the decision to limit the increase to 5 percent was based on budget proposals in the State Legislature. Under the proposals, the CSU would be provided with state funding to serve as many as 29,000 more students and restore additional class sections.

The Trustees left open the possibility of revisiting the fee levels in November if the state does not provide the funding in the current proposals.

More than 187,000 CSU students will pay no fee increase due to increases in federal, state, and institutional grants, CSU fee waivers and expanded federal tax credits. Financial aid and federal tax credits are available for many students, including those with family incomes of up to $180,000.

The CSU has faced unprecedented cuts in state support over the past two years totaling approximately $625 million, and has implemented a number of measures to address the drastic funding decline including employee furloughs, layoffs, increased student fees, reduced enrollment, and other campus cost-cutting measures.

No one likes to raise fees for our students. However, the reality is that the state of California is not providing the funds needed to ensure quality and access. Until our political leadership makes public higher education a clear priority, it is likely that student fees of various types will continue increasing in future years.

For Humboldt State, it is critical that we do everything possible to maintain a high-quality education and the services students expect. These are our priorities, and they are the reasons that the demand for admission to HSU is higher than ever.

Sincerely,
