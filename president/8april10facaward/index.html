Dear Colleagues and Students:

I hope you will join me at a reception on Thursday, April 15, from 3-4:30 in the Kate Buchanan Room to celebrate the faculty award recipients for the 2009/2010 academic year.

The Outstanding Professor award goes to Professor Jacob Varkey who has made major contributions to the success of many students in the biological sciences. He was responsible for obtaining multiple Howard Hughes Medical Institute grants to support undergraduate education to HSU. Jacob's research on the genetics of nematode worms is well known.

The Scholar of the Year award goes to Professor Matthew Johnson. Matt has a very productive research program and has guided the development of a number of graduate students. He also finds time to lead the Wildlife Department.

The Excellence in Teaching - Lecturer award goes to Professor Jeffrey Dunk who teaches in the Department of Environmental and Natural Resource Sciences. Jeffrey focuses his teaching on critical thinking and helping students to become good citizens and able professionals. He is also a productive scholar working on the forces that affect species distributions and the causes of extraordinary biological diversity.

The Outstanding Service award goes to Professor Marshelle Thobaben. Marshelle has devoted many years to shared governance both on campus and for the CSU system.

These individuals have been identified by their colleagues as among the very strongest faculty we have at HSU. Please join me to celebrate their accomplishments and what they have meant to our university.

Sincerely,

Rollin C. Richmond
President
