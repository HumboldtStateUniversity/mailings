Dear Students,

Humboldt State University offers two significant student loan forgiveness programs for students who plan to become teachers in California.

The Assumption Program of Loans for Education (APLE) is a financial aid program for future teachers in the form of student loan pay-back. It is designed to encourage California undergraduates and credential students to become teachers in "teacher-shortage" areas and high-need schools.

The especially important benefit of the APLE awards is that they repay between $11,000 and $19,000 in outstanding educational loan balances for future teachers. This is in return for four consecutive years of qualifying teaching service in a California K-12 public school.

Currently, more than 3,000 APLE awards remain available for California State University students, but they must be awarded before July, 2011. Juniors and seniors who are interested in teaching, as well as teacher credential students in several fields, are eligible for awards.

If you are a student who plans to become a public school teacher in California and have not yet taken advantage of this program, I urge you to do so. In order to ensure that you receive an APLE 2010-11 award, you should apply now.

You can obtain the easy-to-fill-out application for an APLE award at: www.csusuccess.org/APLE

It is critical that you apply for APLE by June 15, 2011. Doing so will enable you to receive an APLE award this year. It will help you to defer and then pay back your student loans.

In addition, students can also take advantage of the Federal Loan Forgiveness Program that also targets the same high need areas in the nation along with high need subjects, i.e., math, science, and special education. As with the APLE Program, students can receive $5,000 to $17,500 of additional loan forgiveness over a five-year period rather than the four year maximum of the APLE awards. You can find additional information about this federal program by visiting the web site. The combination of the two programs certainly helps defray the overall costs of education by substantial amounts, particularly with science, math, and special education.

I hope you will take advantage of this opportunity immediately and wish you much success in your educational and professional endeavors.
