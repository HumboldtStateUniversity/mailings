CSU AND CFA CONTINUE COLLECTIVE BARGAINING

Faculty Union Considers Concerted Activities Over Portion of Pay Increases Not Paid in 08/09 and 09/10 Due to State Budget Cuts.

This document provides information on collective bargaining between the California State University and the California Faculty Association, the labor union representing university faculty.

Background

In 2007, CSU and CFA reached agreement on a new, three-year contract. Due to significant uncertainty over the state of California's budget, this contract included automatic "reopeners" on compensation issues if certain state funding thresholds were not met. As a result, CSU and CFA returned to the bargaining table over a portion of faculty salary increases. Because this contract expired on June 30, 2010, the two parties are currently negotiating a successor contract at the same time these compensation "re-openers" are negotiated. With impasse and fact-finding procedures now complete on these "re-openers," the faculty union is polling its membership over whether to conduct concerted activities, including the threat of a one-day strike, over CSU's decision not to implement the faculty union's full wage demands under the previous contract.

Salary Increases Received by Faculty: 2008-2010

  • On June 30, 2008, a General Salary increase
    • Total cost: $31.3 million
    • No other employee group received a GSI in 2008
  • In 2009, Post-Promotion Increases to full professor and equivalent lecturers, counselors, librarians and coach ranks
    • Half of the awards were paid in February 2009 and half in July 2009
    • Total ongoing cost: $13.3 million
  • In 2008/09 and 2009/10, promotions, range elevations and market increases
    • Total ongoing cost: $12.5 million
  • In September 2010, a General Salary Increase of 0.045%
    • Total ongoing cost: $700,000
    • Source of funds was leftover uncommitted resources from PPI program. CFA chose to provide GSI to everybody, rather than PPI to eligible
  • In Fall 2010, equity increases went to associate and full professor ranks
    • Retroactive to July 1, 2008
    • Total ongoing cost: $1.25 million
    • Source of funds were uncommitted resources from Year 1 of equity program

Total Dollar Value of CFA Salary Increases 2008-2010 = $59.05 million.

Additional CFA Salary Demands 2008-2010

In both 2008/09 and 2009/2010, the CFA's demands were the same:

  • In 2008/09, a 2.65% Service Salary Increase to eligible members (about 40% of the faculty) plus $7 million for an equity program for associate and full professors
    • Total cost: $23.3 million
  • In 2009/10, a 2.65% SSI for eligible faculty members, plus $6 million for an equity program for associate and full professors
    • Total cost: $20.6 million
    • Lower cost than 2008/09 because about half of the increase would have been one-time bonuses

Comparison to Other Employee Groups: 2008-2010

  • In 2008/09, Management Personnel Plan (MPP) employees received increases for equity, reassignment or promotion, but no General Salary Increases
    • Total ongoing cost: $3.33 million
  • In 2009/2010, increases for equity reassignment or promotion
    • Total ongoing cost: $2.39 million

Total dollar value of MPP salary increases 2008-2010 = $5.72 million

  • In 2008/09, CSUEU employees received increases for in-range progressions, promotions, and performance increases, but no General Salary Increases
    • Total ongoing cost: $5.95 million
  • In 2009/10, increase for in-range progressions, promotions and performance increases
    • Total ongoing cost: $2.32 million

Total dollar value of CSUEU salary increases 2008-2010 = $8.27 million

  • No sitting CSU president, chancellor or vice chancellors have received a salary increase since 2007
  • No other employee group has received a General Salary Increase from 2008 to present. There have been individual promotions or in-class range adjustments, but no across-the-board increases.

State Funding for CSU: 2008-2010

  • State general fund support dropped from $2.97 billion in 07/08 to $2.46 billion in 09/10, a budget cut of $510 million.
  • The faculty lost more than 1,200 full time equivalent positions or about 2,700 individuals
  • Fall student enrollment dropped by almost 18,000 full time equivalent students
  • Virtually all employee groups took a 10% pay cut in the form on furloughs, including presidents and executives
  • CSU was forced to raise tuition by almost $1,000 to address the massive budget cut.

What is CFA Calling a Potential Strike Over?

The faculty union and the CSU could not come to an agreement regarding equity increases for 08/09 and 09/10. The increases represent an ongoing cost of $20 million and affect 40% of the faculty. This is the ONLY issue that CFA can legally strike over.

Concurrently, CFA and CSU have been in collective bargaining over a successor contract to the one that expired on June 30, 2011. The CFA cannot legally strike over issues that the two sides are still bargaining over.

The faculty union has asked for the same equity increases to be paid in the successor contract that were not paid in 08/09 and 09/10.

The $20 million CFA is contemplating striking over would pay for 315 full-time temporary lecturer jobs, enrollment of 2,800 students or 3,150 course sections.

What Is CSU Proposing in the Successor Contract?

CSU is currently negotiating with the faculty union for a contract to succeed the one that expired in June 2010. CSU has not proposed ANY reductions in employee salaries, health care benefits or pensions for faculty.

What CSU has proposed include:

  • An evaluation process before lecturers receive defacto tenure employment
  • Not paying for the release time of the faculty union president and political action chair to conduct union business
  • Continuing to offer summer classes within extended education so that students can enroll in needed classes to graduate
  • Requiring faculty to report outside employment consistent with a recommendation of the Bureau of State Audits

What Are the Next Steps?

CFA is apparently conducting a vote of their members about support for a faculty strike on one or more days at one or more campuses. They have targeted November 17 as a potential strike day at either Dominguez Hills and/or East Bay campuses.

The CSU will be providing information to campuses in the event that students' classes are cancelled, disruptions in operations and other issues as the faculty union counts its votes.

Bargaining on the successor contract will continue until both sides agree to declare an impasse, and proceed to mediation and fact finding.
