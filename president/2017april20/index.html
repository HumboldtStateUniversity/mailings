April 20, 2017
Care and Support in Our HSU Community

Dear HSU Alumni,

As you may know, Humboldt State University endured a terrible loss with the death of David Josiah (“DJ”) Lawson, a 19-year-old student. He was stabbed and killed during a gathering in Arcata early Saturday morning.

We are all struggling to cope with this tragedy, and we are all grieving. I know that many of you, as a part of our Humboldt State University Community, are experiencing the pain of this loss, as well. You are in our thoughts, and I ask that you send your best wishes in Humboldt’s direction.

Last night, Josiah’s family arrived in Arcata, and the family is meeting with a number of us today. They are, of course, devastated. They are also remarkably caring as they have reached out to comfort many of those who knew Josiah.

At the family’s request, we will be holding a Celebration of Life this evening on campus, an event that is being planned by Josiah’s friends, including members of HSU’s Brothers United. This gathering will begin at 5 p.m. in the Kate Buchanan Room. We will continue to assist the family members in any way we can.

Our focus now, and in the weeks to come, will remain on supporting our students. This support will continue to include individual help with classes, counseling, small-group discussions, and larger events open to the entire campus.

As a University community, we mourn this loss of a bright young student, and we join together to support Josiah’s family, friends, and all those who have been touched by his life.


Sincerely yours,

Lisa A. Rossbacher, Ph.D.
President
