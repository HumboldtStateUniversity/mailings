Dear Colleagues and Students:

We are fortunate to live in a gorgeous place, and to work and study at an excellent university. However, California is subject to regular earthquakes, some of which are severe. It is important that all of us, especially those of us who work with students, be prepared to tell people what to do and act appropriately ourselves if a significant earthquake does occur.

We need to practice what to do, so please join me this Thursday, October 21 at 10:21a.m., in participating in the Great California ShakeOut Drill (www.shakeout.org).

At 10:21 on Thursday, each of us needs to drop to the ground or floor, cover ourselves by getting under a table or desk, or if outside protect your head with an arm, and hold on. You can visit the following site to see videos prepared by HSU Geology Professor, Dr. Lori Dengler, to see exactly what to do: http://www.humboldt.edu/emergencymgmtprogram/

Also at that site, you can obtain good summaries of how to respond to a number of other emergencies.

I am especially concerned that our faculty and all those who work with groups of students be prepared to help students in the event of an earthquake while they are teaching or working with students. Students will take their cues on how to behave from what you do. Please include information in your syllabi and practice a ShakeOut with students in each of your classes.

If anyone has suggestions or ideas about the ShakeOut on our campus, please send an email to shakeout@humboldt.edu.

Thanks for practicing and preparing, and let's hope we don't need it.


Rollin C. Richmond
President
