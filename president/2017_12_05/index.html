December 5, 2017
Donors Help HSU Preserve Football

Dear Lumberjack Football Players and Family Members,

Earlier today, I met with student-athletes, coaches, and others to share the good news that Humboldt State University will continue fielding our football team. Football will remain a part of our Division II Intercollegiate Athletics program, along with all of our other sports.

Today is a good day for Lumberjack Football!

This outcome was made possible by the tremendous outpouring of support from alumni and community donors. A group of boosters worked very hard to generate financial commitments to help bolster our Athletics budget, which has been in deficit the last three years. They assured me that they had more than $500,000 in commitments for year one, and that they could immediately begin helping us collect payments. I am so very appreciative.

Significantly, this group of boosters is also committed to helping fund Athletics for additional years, with the goal of at least $500,000 each year for five years. Many of those who made commitments during the fund drive indicated they were willing to continue giving for that time. This continued support will be important, as the University as a whole is dealing with a structural deficit, and is not in a position to allocate additional funds to Athletics.

I know that many of you wanted news of this decision earlier, and I apologize for that not being possible. We were working very hard to achieve a positive outcome for football. In this case, that meant taking extra time, having additional conversations, and being flexible.

To our players, I promise you that HSU will do everything possible to support you. Some of you have begun considering a transfer. We hope you will reconsider, and that you will play next year in the green and gold for Humboldt State.

To our parents and family members, I thank you for entrusting your student-athlete to HSU. We are proud of them, and we are grateful to you for your support of Lumberjack football.

Sincerely,

Lisa A. Rossbacher, Ph.D.
President
