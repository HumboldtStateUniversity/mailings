Dear Colleagues:

As you probably know, California has been successful in getting additional education stimulus funds from the federal government, and the CSU has been allocated $106 million. These funds have been included as part of our 2010-11 budget allocation by the Governor and Legislature. Note that these funds are one-time only, and the CSU will need to press our state government to replace them with base funds as the budget for 2011-12 is developed.

Humboldt State University will receive $2,514,300 of the $106 million, based upon our enrollment target for this year.

The Vice Presidents and I prepared a proposal for the allocation of HSU's proportion of these funds, and have reviewed that proposal with the University Executive Committee, which approved it unanimously. Here are the details of how these one-time funds will be allocated and used in our current-year budget.

Academic Affairs: $2,039,300

These funds will be used to provide classes for an additional 200-500 students expected to enroll for the Spring semester. Any funds remaining at the end of the semester will be carried over for use in Fall 2011.

Administrative Affairs: $300,000

A portion of these funds will be used to refill the vacant Energy Manager/Chief Engineer position ($130,000). This position is critical to HSU's sustainability initiative, particularly in focusing on reducing greenhouse gas emissions and HSU's carbon footprint, and for handling increased regulatory reporting. Approximately $80,000 will be used to hire a part-time administrative analyst specialist and a part-time light automotive equipment operator. These expenditures allow us to create a full-time sustainability coordinator. The remaining funds ($90,000) will be used to replace two custodians lost to previous budget reductions to help care for our new buildings.

Student Affairs: $130,000

These funds will be used as follows:

  • $25,000 - Student Assistants in Registrar's Office
  • $5,000 - Financial Aid/Scholarship coordination
  • $18,000 - SDRC staffing and software, time-base increase and hourly intermittent help with accommodations
  • $23,000 - Backfill Registrar's Office to allow full-time position in Veteran's Affairs
  • $55,000 - Hobson's CRM module for student communication

Advancement: $45,000

These funds will be used as follows:

  • $22,000 - Campus calendar project, six-month start-up phase
  • $8,000 - Mobile campus web app project
  • $15,000 - Centennial preparation including forums, development of communications plan, and graphic identity work

Should you have questions about the use of these funds, please contact the appropriate Vice President's Office for further information. I hope your semester is going well.

Sincerely,


Rollin C. Richmond
President
