Welcome to Spring Semester

Welcome back for the Spring Semester at Humboldt State University! I hope you had a wonderful winter break and enjoyed your time with family and friends.

The arrival of students and start of classes is a perfect reminder of the very important, and inspiring, mission that we all share. We offer students access to affordable, high-quality education that is responsive to the needs of a rapidly changing world. And we prepare graduates to be responsible members of diverse societies. More simply, as I wrote to students earlier this week, the faculty and staff here are rooting for them and working hard to help them have great educational experiences.

I hope you were able to join your colleagues for the annual Spring Welcome event last week and to spend time celebrating the dedicated service of our faculty and staff. A real highlight was presenting this year’s staff award recipients – a group of people who are great at their jobs and who care deeply about our students. The 10 recipients this year are:

  • Jana Ashbrook from Housing and Dining Services
  • Stacy Becker from the Service Learning Center
  • Travis Brunner from the Office of the Registrar
  • Mary Comella from the Departments of Chemistry and of Physics & Astronomy
  • Teresa Cox from Facilities Management
  • Mira Friedman from the Health Center
  • Greg Granoff from the Music Department
  • Jeff Robison from Facilities Management
  • John Smith from Facilities Management
  • Mary Ann Brown from Dining Services

Please take time to congratulate these colleagues personally if you have a chance.

Also at the Spring Welcome, I mentioned five “C’s” that I think will be important as we collectively plan for our future: Connect, Communicate, Conserve, Create, and Celebrate.

Of these, I think that connecting and communicating are perhaps most vital. One of the things that has so impressed me as I get to know the campus is the strong sense of community at Humboldt State. It is one of the things we are known for and something that alumni tell me they value about their time at Humboldt. I believe we need to stay focused on maintaining and strengthening this sense of community, and I believe we can use it as a foundation for achieving our other goals.

I look forward to visiting with you this spring and learning more about the projects and activities in your area. Have a great semester!

With best wishes,

LAR
Lisa A. Rossbacher, Ph.D.
