August 20, 2018
Welcome to Fall Semester

Dear Students,

Welcome to a new academic year at Humboldt State University!

Whether you are returning to campus or arriving for your first year – welcome. With move-in and orientation last week, and classes starting this week, HSU is definitely re-energized. I wish you all the best in your classes, as well as in your research, creative projects, performances, presentations, sports teams, clubs, and other activities.

In addition to focusing on your studies, you have many opportunities to become involved in campus and community life during your time at HSU. We have concerts, lectures, clubs, recreational sports, volunteer activities, and more – including programs like Youth Educational Services (better known as Y.E.S.), which is celebrating its 50th year. Our region of the Redwood Coast also has an amazing natural environment that is perfect for outdoor adventures. Taking advantage of some of these opportunities will make your HSU experience even more special, and you may discover new interests that you hadn’t considered before. I certainly did when I attended college; I started out as an English major and then discovered geology!

As you walk around campus, you’ll notice work in progress at the Library and the Theatre Arts Building, as well as new temporary offices, classrooms, and services on the Campus Events Field. We’ve done all we can to minimize the inconvenience, but there’s no way to avoid some impact of these renovations. Please know this work is important, with upgrades and seismic safety improvements being made to both buildings.

I will also share with you that HSU recently completed a successful reaccreditation process. This may not be very exciting to most students, but being an accredited university is important. Accreditation is the way that higher education assesses itself, and it is how we demonstrate our value to students and their families, legislators, and the general public.

Finally, I want to note that the land where Humboldt State is located is part of the ancestral homeland of the Wiyot people. The Wiyot lived here more than a thousand years before this University was founded. Because so much of an HSU education is characterized by a sense of place, acknowledging the history of this land and our indebtedness to the Wiyot people is significant.

Thank you for choosing to be a part of this wonderful HSU community. I look forward to following your progress and hearing about your successes during the upcoming year.

With best wishes,

Lisa A. Rossbacher, Ph.D.
President
