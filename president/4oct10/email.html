Dear HSU Campus Community,

Child abuse touches everyone's life. We all know someone who was abused or neglected as a child, even if that person has never told us about it.

One organization that does important work helping these children is CASA (Court Appointed Special Advocates) of Humboldt. CASA provides a trained volunteer advocate to stand by the child in court and in life. When a child is removed from a home, CASA steps in and helps the child navigate the confusing waters of the Foster Care system.

Now, CASA needs our help, and a group of HSU students is organizing a response.

The HSU CASA Kid Walk Team is asking HSU students, faculty and staff to help overcome a 66 percent cut in state funding of CASA of Humboldt by participating in the first "HSU Day of Texting" on Wednesday, October 6.

To participate, members of the HSU community are encouraged to text "CASA" to 20222, thus making a $10 contribution to CASA of Humboldt, which will appear on the next month's cell phone bill (standard text messaging rates apply).

Up to three texts can be made from each phone and all funds raised will stay in Humboldt County to advocate for abused and neglected children. All texts made on October 6 will be credited to the HSU "Day of Texting" event and the HSU Kid Walk Team. College of the Redwoods is hosting its own "Day of Texting" event and has challenged HSU to a friendly competition to see which school can generate the most texts.

This really is a good cause, and HSU has a long history of supporting CASA's work. There are internships, club activities and educational outreach, and our campus currently has 11 advocates actively enrolled.

In Humboldt County, child abuse is reported at a rate nearly two times the state average. A foster child who is assigned a CASA volunteer will spend less time in foster care and 90 percent of children appointed a CASA volunteer do not reenter the child welfare system after their case is closed.

CASA of Humboldt is currently able to provide an advocate for less than half of Humboldt's abused and neglected children, but we know there are enough caring and qualified people in our community to help them all. We just need your help to find, train, support and retain these very special volunteers. By providing foster children with a CASA volunteer, we give the child a more stable foundation to grow and become healthy, contributing members of our community.

If your class schedule permits, we would appreciate it if you could take three minutes of your class time on October 6 to show the informative video at http://kidwalk.humboldtcasa.org/ textingday2010HSU.php and allow your students to take out their cell phones as a class and make a text contribution.

Student volunteers for CASA will be in the quad from 9-6 on Texting Day, October 6, and will be passing out "I texted CASA" stickers to all who have texted. Please consider taking part.

On behalf of the children,


Rollin C. Richmond
