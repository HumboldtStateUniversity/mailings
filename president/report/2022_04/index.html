President's Report
April
2022
Since our transition to a polytechnic university became official, I’ve had the honor to connect with a wide variety of community and Tribal groups to share our vision and also explore greater opportunities to partner and discuss shared interests, goals and priorities.
Provost Jenn Capps and I recently met with the Humboldt Association of Realtors to discuss our enrollment and hiring growth expectations, and to share ideas to ensure housing is available for the community. Earlier I met with the Arcata Chamber of Commerce, the business leaders of our community, many of whom are Humboldt alumni. I also had a very positive meeting with the community leaders who make up the President’s Community Advisory Committee.
In early April, College of the Redwoods President Keith Flamer, Provost Jenn Capps and I visited the Hoopa Valley Tribal Nation headquarters and met with the leaders of the regional Tribal Nations to learn more about how Cal Poly Humboldt can partner to support the educational dreams of their members. These visits are designed to honor the sovereignty of each Tribal Nation through a reciprocal relationship, characterized by listening and sharing. College of the Redwoods has an established center and relationship with the Hoopa Valley Tribe, on which both institutions may continue to build in service to the Tribe.
President Flamer and I are also building on the work of the Native American Advisory Council (NAAC), which was re-established by former Cal Poly Humboldt President Lisa Rossbacher, to foster a Tribal government-to-university relationship. This vital connection to the Native American communities prioritizes the development of leader-to-leader relationships through one-on-one meetings and visits to Tribal headquarters, attendance as invited guests at Tribal events, participation in specific Northcoast Tribal Leaders Association meetings, and hosting an annual spring campus meeting.
This leader-to-leader relationship is an expression of President Flamer’s and my respect for the sovereignty of tribal nations and their desire to listen to and speak directly with elected leaders. Based on insights from Tribal leaders and others about the higher education trajectories and persistent structural challenges impacting local Native students, the new joint NAAC will serve as an external advisory group of Tribal leaders for the Presidents of the College of the Redwoods and Cal Poly Humboldt.
Many people have been concerned by global events recently, and I am reminded that we are part of a global community, rich in opportunities and experiences, that help educate our students.
We recently hosted International Education Week, an annual opportunity to explore, learn about and celebrate our connections to the world beyond our local and national boundaries.
Mary Akpovi, the keynote speaker, was born overseas, but gained her education and has spent her professional life here in California. Throughout her experiences, she has maintained her global outlook through volunteer work overseas where she has been instrumental in founding schools in three African countries that support vocational education for women.
Additionally, our campus and greater community were able to hear from Tom Mills from University of Lancaster. He shared about Latin America from the British point of view, and Baroness Suttie of Hawick, a member of the House of Lords in London and leading political expert on Russia.
Students were also offered career advice and guidance. The Peace Corps representative, the new diplomat in residence from the State Department, and our own Academic Career and Advising Center gave tips and practical steps about working overseas. Humboldt has been recognized many times as one of the Peace Corps’ top volunteer-producing universities.
And as always, I encourage students to explore our study abroad programs. They are often more affordable and within greater reach than people expect, and can create opportunities and experiences that last a lifetime.
Finally, I invite you to learn more about our continued polytechnic implementation, a campus-wide effort with buy-in from our entire community. Learn more, give feedback, and get involved by visiting humboldt.edu/polytechnic.

HSU Joins California’s Historic College Service Program

California recently launched the largest state-level investment in a college service program in California history: #CaliforniansForAll College Corps, which will help create debt-free college pathways for low-income students who commit to serve. Cal Poly Humboldt will be awarded up to $3.3 million to support approximately 100 students each year for their community projects, and to support community partners that host students over the next two academic years, starting in Fall 2022.
