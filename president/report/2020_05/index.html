President's Report
May
2020
Our communities have certainly been put to the test over the last two months.
During that time, I’ve been inspired by the resiliency of HSU students. They have stepped up to support their families, friends, and communities, all while adapting to unprecedented changes themselves.
I’m also proud of our faculty and staff, who succeeded in the massive undertaking of converting to virtual instruction, support, and operations. They continue to work hard to protect health and safety, and to ensure our students make progress toward their degree. Many have gone above and beyond their normal jobs. Campus community members have donated cloth face coverings for students. Donors have lent support to help with new technology needs of students as well as basic needs like food and housing. IT staff and librarians, custodians and food service workers, student athletes and vets, instructors and support staff, campus leaders and community members -- all of them and many others have pitched in to help us through this pandemic.
Thank you to all of you!
Students will continue to need support with basic needs, technology, and more, and we expect an increase in the need for scholarships in coming years. Donor support makes a real difference. If you are interested in helping, there are a number of funds to consider on the Humboldt Connected giving site.
There is an amazing sense of community and positive spirit on campus. Maybe it’s the sunshine or As we look ahead, one thing we’re focused on is the status of instruction this fall. I assure you that the faculty and staff at Humboldt State are working very hard on this - and that we will have a fall and spring semester. Intense planning for a number of possibilities is underway. We are not delaying, and we will be making important short-term changes while also re-imagining what HSU can be in a post-COVID world.
Humboldt State isn’t alone in this. The question of how to conduct the fall semester is the biggest topic in U.S. higher education right now. Governing boards are struggling with it. Universities are having long and often frustrating internal discussions about the options. Current and incoming students are definitely asking for more information.
None of us know what the “new normal” will look like or even when we will get there. What we do know is that no matter what happens during the next months and years, Humboldt State will remain focused on offering a positive, meaningful educational experience. We are clear-eyed and mission-focused, and we are committed to our students and this community.
Finally, please help our Class of 2020 celebrate on May 16 at the virtual commencement ceremony. Learn more about the celebration at the commencement website.
— President Jackson
