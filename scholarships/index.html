Dear Friends,

When we first heard about the tragic bus accident on April 10th we were all shaken to the core. Hearts ached. Then there was an immediate outpouring of support-- people came together to support the emergency response effort, connect families, visit the injured in the hospital, be there for all involved, and even to turn tragedy into a Preview day focused on accomplishing educational goals, in honor of those we lost. I have never been more proud of our Humboldt State community.

Many of us also wished to do more, to support and honor those affected in a lasting way.

In response, Humboldt State University has established two scholarships to support low-income, first generation college students. The Arthur Arzola Memorial Scholarship honors HSU's admissions counselor who worked passionately to help students attain a college education. The Michael Myvette and Mattison Haywood Memorial Scholarship honors the recently engaged alumni couple who were chaperones on the trip. All three were among those killed in the accident.

We all know the power of higher education. It transforms individuals, families and communities. I'm thankful for this direct and powerful way for each of us to positively change the lives of students through education in honor of Arthur, Michael and Mattison. All three of them were committed to expanding access to higher education. Let's rally together as a Humboldt Family by making a gift to one or both of these scholarships.

You can double the impact of your generosity by making your gift before midnight, April 25th. Make a gift to either (or both) of these funds, and the HSU Advancement Foundation will match contributions up to $20,000.

Make your gift today!

You can also help out on this day of giving by sharing the memorial scholarships link on Facebook or Twitter. And you can go to this Friday's Dance of the Century, where the Advancement Foundation will give $5 to the scholarship funds for each of the first 1,000 people who attend.

I appreciate your help. Let's turn heartbreak into hope for as many bright young people as possible.

Most sincerely,

Romi
Romi Hitchcock Tinseth
Assistant Director of Admissions
Humboldt State University
